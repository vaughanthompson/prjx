Our Purpose: It's our people that keep our customers going


Thanks to all our people across TDX who help keep our customers going. It's this mindset of putting our customers first, that has made us successful over the past 40-plus years.

Just one example of this is Leon Proctor, who took a call at home afterhours from SteelServe. Not only did he take the call, he went above and beyond and delivered the part they needed which meant they were able to sort the issue and keep their machines running on a Saturday night.

There is so much that goes unseen across our business, but it's the sum of all these examples of going above and beyond that sets TDX apart from our competitors and delivers a world-class experience for our customers.

Last month we released the newly created TDX purpose and values. You can watch the video here if you missed it. Again, thanks to everyone for their input, we have landed with a great purpose and set of values that will give us clarity day in and day out.

Our six values are those deeply held beliefs or behaviours that we need to live every day. Our values are:

  1.   We care, take care: Our people will return home in the same condition that they came to work
  2.   Be real, commit, deliver: We turn up for work with the right mindset
  3.   We're in it together: We know that we are better when we work together
  4.   We will sort it: We challenge the status quo and have an eye on tomorrow
  5.   Our customer's success is our business: We will understand our customer better than anybody else
  6.   We will be the partner of choice: We will treat our partners as we would like to be treated

We need everyone to commit to these values, they need to be more than words on a bit of paper. Over the coming months our senior management team will be helping us to roll them out across the business.

It is great to welcome Linde Material Handling into our TDX family. Linde is a world-class brand that will complement what we already offer, while opening us up to new exciting opportunities and customers.

We became the Linde dealer for New Zealand from November 13 and it's been a journey getting to this day. A huge vote of thanks needs to go to Joe Gradwell for leading this launch project, for more information check out Joe's update in this newsletter.

Speaking of new additions to the TDX team, please welcome Phillip Bracefield as the GM of Supply Chain. We featured Phillip in last month's newsletter and he officially started October 30, please say hi to Phillip when you see him around the office.

Everyone involved in the recent Volvo management team visit from Sweden also needs to be recognised, thanks to your participation it really made their trip incredibly memorable.

Whilst it takes your attention away from the day-to-day tasks, opportunities like this are worth their weight in gold. It gives their team insight into what we're about, our customers as well as how their machines are used here in New Zealand and the focus we give to the Volvo brand.

Again, it ties back to communication and relationships. The Volvo team left for home knowing more, understanding more and appreciating more about TDX.

As mentioned at the start, it's our people that keep our customers going. Thanks to every one of you for playing your part, we have so much to be proud of here at TDX.

Thanks,
Colm.

 

NPS an important part of improving customer experience at TDX

At TDX, the customer is at the centre of everything we do - it's our aim to deliver an exceptional experience every time right across the business. It's in our customer tagline: a 'World class experience' backed by 'Local know-how.'


To help keep our focus on this promise, we introduced a measure called 'Net Promoter Score' or NPS a few years ago, it's a great way to track our customers' experience with TDX.

NPS is a survey for our customers to provide direct feedback, for us to see how we're doing and also identify areas we can aim to improve. This also helps us to keep one step ahead of our competitors, evolving and enhancing the experience our customers have when dealing with our brand.

The NPS programme emails a survey to a customer after they deal with TDX. These include when we have delivered a new machine to a customer, serviced a machine or when one of our team has a face-to-face meeting with a customer.

Results demonstrate that overall we are delivering great experiences to our customers, but there's always room for improvement (see some stats below).

A key theme throughout the NPS results demonstrate that the customers are wanting us to 'keep them informed.' In most cases, the more we keep the customer updated, the better their experience with TDX.

Think about it from your point of view: if you had an issue with a new big ticket item arriving or being serviced that you had bought - a TV, fridge or car - there's nothing more frustrating than not hearing back from the place you're dealing with, especially if you have to start chasing them up.

It's annoying, it's bad customer service and it's just the same in our game.

The only way to deliver exceptional customer experiences is to listen to our customers and learn from the good wins or when things don't go so right. Both are important.

To help provide more visibility of NPS results, we're committed to sharing NPS promoter scores on a weekly basis on Viva, how we are tracking on NPS through branch meetings and setting up PowerBI reports. This will allow all of us to see how we're tracking and how we can help continue to build the relationships with our customers and make their experience with TDX an even better one.

NPS questions we ask our customers.

Q1. Based on your experience, how likely would you recommend TDX to friends or family? 10 (very likely), 0 (not likely).

10-9 scores - promoters
7-8 scores - passives
0-6 scores - detractors

Q2. What was the main reason for your score?
Pricing / Value, Customer Service / Communication, Product Range / Quality, Something Else

Q3 Let us know how we can improve things in relation to (topic from Q2)?

Key NPS Stats

Number of surveys received this year: 1,264

Overall NPS score: 61.1% (% promoter scores - % detractor scores).
Benchmark is 40%. (885 promoter scores, 266 passive scores and 113 detractor scores)

    Top topics raised by customers (Q2):
  • Customer Service / Communication - 50% of all responses
  • Pricing and Value - 11% of all responses
  • Something Else - 11% of all responses
  • Product Range/ Quality - 6% of all responses

 

Linde helping lift TDX to a whole new level


TDX is a business on the move forward and now, with the distributorship of a new brand confirmed, upwards as well.

“TDX has been working with Linde for a number of months, securing the local distributorship for forklifts in the NZ market,” said Joe Gradwell, General Manager Aftersales Operations.

“They're a great brand to have as part of the TDX portfolio as they have an excellent reputation, are reliable, well made and add a level of diversity to our business.

“They're not the cheapest, but the quality is outstanding - just in the same way our Volvo portfolio fills a similar position amongst earthmoving machinery.”

To help meet the demands of the new brand, TDX has been recruiting staff to join the team.

“One incredibly encouraging consideration is that there have been a massive number of applicants for the new TDX Linde roles we've advertised,” said Joe.

“There are 12 roles to start with, at my most recent count we've received more than 270 applications.

“There is a huge number of people wanting to jump on board with TDX, they see what we do and how we go about our business plus the relationship we have with our customers. That's proving to be a major drawcard in attracting people to join our team.”

Another important consideration associated with Linde relates to sustainability initiatives, as the brand offers a number of battery-electric options.

“These emissions-free models align with our Toitu initiative and will help us on our carbon measurement and emission reduction journey,” said Joe.

The recently announced partnership went live on November 13 and, over time, plans are for it to become a separate business operating alongside TDX, adding new people to the team as it expands.

“It's exciting to be part of a new distributorship such as this one with Linde, it's really got us scaling new heights,” added Joe.

Check our our new LDX website at ldx.co.nz or our new launch video here.

Hero Staff Member:
Joel Jones,
TDX Palmerston North


This month's Hero a Staff Member features Joel Jones from TDX Palmerston North and his recent experience with a particular customer. Joel's example is told by Karl Scragg, Business Improvements Manager.


Joel hasn't been with TDX particularly long, literally only three months, but he's already starting to make real inroads building excellent relationships with customers in the Palmerston North branch.

Mike Haycock Contracting has been a TDX customer for about four years, he has a few pieces of Volvo equipment, spread across two regions and was relying on the Napier team to help him out with any issues.

I took a call from Mike about a problem he was having with some gear, Joel was tasked to visit the customer. A short time after Joel had left the site, I saw Mike's number come up on my phone - I wondered what he could be ringing me about.

I answered and Mike wouldn't let me get a word in, he was so full of praise for the job Joel had done, finishing with “You have a good man there and I am over the moon with the service I received.”

For us at TDX Joel has applied our purpose which is “It's our people that keep our customers going.”

He has also applied the following values in achieving this just with his nature and professional approach which underpins these values:

  1.   Be Real, Commit and Deliver - Joel was himself, turned up to work with the right mindset for the day
  2.   We will sort it - Joel had a timeline and a task and delivered on it
  3.   Our Customers Success is our business - Joel understands the concept that if we have successful customers that this pays back to the business.



Based on Mike's experience with Joel, they're very strongly in the TDX camp - I wouldn't be surprised if they decide to buy some more machinery sometime in the near future - and they're very happy to have Palmerston North as a go-to branch.

Congratulations Joel for a job well done, keep up the good work!

Introduction. End of Financial Year, Wrap up and Goals. By Phil Bracefield, COO.



Hi Team,

As we end one financial year and start another, it's important to pause and reflect on what has been a significant period for TDX. Off the back of a challenging time, the progress we have made together is something every one of us should be proud of.

It is particularly pleasing to recognise the excellent safety record we have achieved over the past 12-months with no serious harm. It reflects our value to take care and our commitment that if you see something, say something and do something.

Equally encouraging is the financial contribution from across all areas of TDX. Our bottom-line financial performance has strengthened steadily throughout the financial year and it's the collective effort of everyone in the business.

Our Equipment Sales division is performing well, with good bottom line results in January and February. We are forecasting an even stronger equipment sales month in March, which included the sales of the first Fuchs machine to C3 in Tauranga after a successful trial period with that customer. It's been great to see the Sales and Service team working together closely to get machines out of the door! Looking ahead, April and May are also shaping up to be strong months.

Shell have had a great year and, alongside our wider Aftersales Support Team, have been energised by the recent sales conference in Christchurch. Despite ongoing competitive challenges and current global events, our product lines have held their ground and performed solidly.

Our Aftersales and support teams, such as, service technicians, product support, warehouse, supply chain, admin and finance teams, continue to set the benchmark for industry-leading support. Their commitment truly reflects our belief that it really is “our people who keep our customers going”. Our Parts teams have also delivered an outstanding performance throughout this year and should be equally proud of their customer service and proactive communication.

Power products have been consistent in providing returns for the business. While NZ Frost Fans have had a temporary downturn with the wine industry, generation and other customers have remained strong.

Lastly, I would like to specifically acknowledge the LDX team, which has gained real momentum and has had excellent financial results coming into the end of the financial year. Thank you everyone.

On behalf of the Executive Leadership Team and Board, I would like to thank you all very much for an outstanding year. We have started rolling out our state of the nation branch meetings and the team has enjoyed getting around the branchs.

Overall, this has been a strong year built on professionalism, hard work and a genuine One Team approach. We continue to honour what we do well, while embracing innovation and new technology to move us forward.

Finally, congratulations to all team members who have reached personal and professional milestones this year. Your contributions matter.

As we head into the new financial year we know the current conflict in the Middle East will create some challenges, particularly in relation to rising fuel prices and supply. The encouraging thing for me is that we have plans in place to react and the right people ready to meet the challenges ahead. Thank you all for your continued effort and commitment.

Phil

 

LDX Update


Over the past few weeks, it's been all hands on deck at LDX, with John, Andrew, and their teams successfully completing the PDI of 16 new Linde trucks for GIB—11 destined for Christchurch and 5 for Tauranga.

We're pleased to share that the first five trucks were delivered to GIB Christchurch on Monday, with the remainder of the order scheduled for delivery later this year. These units represent the latest in European-spec engineering and come equipped with advanced safety and performance features. Highlights include the Linde Safety Pilot (LSP), which offers a range of customer-customisable operating parameters designed to enhance safe use. Additional safety enhancements include red/green approach lights, upgraded LED lighting (including VertiLight), and an automatic battery isolation system.

The trucks are also fitted with the Linde FleetFOCUS telematics system, enabling GIB to monitor critical machine data such as system diagnostics, alerts, and operator behaviour—supporting both safety and operational efficiency.

In further positive news, Linde/LDX has secured a second order of 17 units for GIB, scheduled to arrive later this year. This reinforces a strong pipeline of activity and reflects GIB's ongoing commitment to upgrading its fleet across key sites in Christchurch and Tauranga.

These developments position Linde/LDX strongly to deepen our relationship with GIB, while also creating valuable opportunities for ongoing aftermarket support, including parts and service. Overall, this is a significant step forward in building long-term value for both LDX and Linde.

Volvo Forestry Excavators Launched at Taupō


We recently introduced our new range of Volvo Forestry Excavators at a customer launch event held at our Taupō branch, and the response was extremely positive.

The event was hosted with support from Product Specialist Ewen Satherley, who provided hands-on demonstrations and insights into how the machines are purpose-built for New Zealand forestry conditions. Local customers were invited to get up close, ask questions, and try out the new machines for themselves — an approach that proved highly effective.

The launch was a successful event, generating a strong level of engagement and sales leads. Customers responded particularly well to the forestry-specific design features, robust build, operator comfort, and Volvo's well-known focus on safety, productivity, and uptime.

Given the positive feedback and commercial interest generated, we're now planning to replicate the launch event at our Nelson branch, extending the opportunity to customers in the upper South Island.




A big thank you to the Taupō team and to Ewen for helping deliver a well-run and impactful launch. We're excited about the opportunities this new Volvo forestry range brings to both our customers and the business.

Hero Staff Member - Jacinda Miller. Keeping Things Moving.


Over the past four years, Jacinda Miller has become an integral part of the TDX team, consistently demonstrating dedication, capability and an unwavering focus on customer service.


Jacinda began her journey as a Service Advisor supporting Palmerston North and Wellington. Since then, her role has grown significantly, and she now serves as Group Service Advisor across Napier, Palmerston North, and Wellington. This is an achievement that reflects both her skill and the trust placed in her.

In a role where no two days are the same, Jacinda thrives on being busy. Her plate is always full, balancing a wide range of responsibilities with ease. From scheduling work for Palmerston North and Wellington to opening and coordinating all jobs for the Napier branch, she ensures everything runs smoothly behind the scenes.

What truly sets Jacinda apart is her exceptional customer engagement. A natural people person, she builds strong, lasting relationships with both customers and colleagues. Her ability to anticipate job requirements, including pre-empting parts needs, helps minimise downtime and ensures faster turnaround times for customers.

This level of foresight and efficiency makes a real difference to the overall service experience.

Jacinda's role requires versatility, organisation and attention to detail - she delivers on all fronts. She keeps everyone informed, communicates clearly and ensures nothing falls through the cracks. Her proactive approach and commitment to tidying up loose ends mean the wider team can rely on her completely.

Living the TDX values every day, particularly with her strong customer focus, Jacinda is a standout contributor. She is not only a vital member of her immediate team but also a valued asset to the wider business.


Thank you, Jacinda, for everything you do to keep things running seamlessly.

Hero Staff Member - Dan Clavelle


TDX Territory Manager - Equipment for the Lower North Island, Dan Clavelle, has been named this month's 'Hero a Staff Member' recipient, recognised for his outstanding commitment, work ethic and customer-first approach.


Dan's impact has been highlighted by colleagues Joe Gradwell and Richard Papps, who have both seen first-hand the effort he brings to the role every day.

Spending time with Dan in Palmerston North recently, Joe observed the level of care and professionalism he applies, even when things don't go to plan. In the lead-up to a series of customer deliveries, Dan encountered a few challenges, but never allowed them to affect the outcome. Instead, he stepped up, personally preparing and grooming a machine ahead of delivery to ensure it met the highest standard.

“What followed left a lasting impression for me,” said Joe.

“Dan delivered an exceptional handover, clearly explaining the machine and its features while also presenting TDX's full 'wrap-around' service offering, it was so good he nearly had me wanting to buy a digger.

“Dan did such a great job, while taking a full company approach,” Joe noted, describing the experience as a great example and a benchmark across the business on how we should approach handovers and deliver a world-class experience.”

During the delivery the owner also pulled Joe aside and explained that he had been to five of our competitors for pricing also and that Dan was the only one to come back to them quickly, with a professional quote in a timely manner, which in the customers words "it made us feel like TDX valued our business." Well done Dan, a job well done!

Richard Papps also highlighted Dan's resilience in a challenging sales environment.

With his territory experiencing a quiet period over the past 18 months, Dan has remained proactive. He has been consistently engaging customers, building relationships and staying visible in the market.

His willingness to step into the East Coast region during a period of consolidation, despite the increased travel, has further demonstrated his commitment.

“He leads by example,” Richard said, identifying Dan's strong presence at customer events and his readiness to go the extra mile.

“Whether on the road, with customers, or supporting the wider team, Dan embodies the values of TDX. His recognition as 'Hero a Staff Member' is well deserved.”

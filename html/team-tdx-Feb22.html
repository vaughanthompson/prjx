High Five - Michael Corrigan
 

Hi everyone

 

My thoughts are with our Auckland team and anyone affected by the recent flooding events.

I want to lead off by sending my thoughts to our people, your families, friends, customers, and anyone who has been affected by the recent flooding in the Auckland area. Members of the leadership team had a catch-up over the weekend and we are committed to supporting anyone part of our TDX team affected by these events. Please don't hesitate to reach out to your manager or me directly if you need assistance with anything.

Looking back at last year, I'd like to thank everyone for your outstanding efforts.. We finished the year as Number One in the Heavy Equipment category, in addition to seven months in top-spot, which was a huge accomplishment and sets us up well for the coming year. We couldn't have achieved this result without the great efforts of all our people and support of our loyal customers.

Before I move on, I'd like to remind everyone that safety is at the absolute front of everything we do and think about. We all need to play our part ensuring that everyone who comes to work goes home safely at the end of the day. Take a moment to consider your actions, survey your work environment or alert someone if you see a potential issue.

When we think about keeping our people safe, we should consider those contractors, suppliers and customers that visit our branches.


Recently we had an incident in Auckland where a visitor was rightfully asked to put on a high viz while visiting our site.

It is everyone's duty to say something if we see something that is unsafe. Just remember to do it with courtesy and in a friendly manner. In this case, it was the visitor's first day on the job and he wasn't aware of our safety policies.

Linked with safety is communication. At a recent February SLT catch up we made the commitment to provide clearer and more concise communications. We will work openly with our teams. We're going to keep channels open and encourage two-way conversations.

I challenge everyone to meet the year head on, to do our best for our customers. I want us to focus on the basics and ensure we do them well. The market looks strong and, working together, we can continue our success.

Let's keep doing the things our customers know and love about TDX, build on our achievements and exceed expectations.

And if we can make these things happen, good things will come - I'm confident we can continue our winning ways and retain our position as Number One and the great success we have experienced through last year.

Best wishes,
Colm.

National Customer Service Centre team aims to provide a world-class experience


When it comes to providing world-class customer support, our TDX National Customer Service Centre team has lofty aspirations. Utilising local know-how and the renowned TDX 'we're local like you' attitude, they aim to be the best in the business.

The relatively new three-member team, consisting of Jazmin (Jaz) Fuller , Adam Mackie and Clinto Poulose, has a wealth of customer service experience across a range of industries between them and their aim is to make every customer interaction a stress-free and positive one.

Being new to TDX, the team has been quick to learn the various areas of our business and get to grips with key software and systems, with the objective of achieving optimal efficiency.

From there they have focused on key day-to-day operations, such as supporting the consumables team managing the customer relationships of lower value customer (under $5,000 customers) as well as providing leads for higher value customers, promoting and managing transactions off our TradeDoor online e-commerce portal and promoting consumable products and key services like our oil monitoring service and pump-over trucks.




One important function is managing the live chat functions on tdxltd.co.nz which requires a high level of customer focus to ensure the best information is delivered quickly and efficiently.

With an eye on the future, the team will be key to delivering even greater levels of customer experience, aligned with other new initiatives across the TDX business.
 

The TDX Supply Chain team is committed to doing a solid job for our customers.


If you Google 'what is a supply chain,' one of the first answers is 'the sequence of processes involved in the production and distribution of a commodity.' To get a better understanding of what's really involved, we had a chat with TDX Supply Chain Manager, James Makoni.

“Supply Chain logistics is a huge part of the TDX business, our anticipation of what our customers will need in the future enables us to stock parts which will help keep their machinery moving when things don't go according to plan.

“There are often long lead times involved, we might order a part but won't see it for six to 12 months, so it's really important we have good communication with Sales to understand plans and place timely orders with overseas suppliers.”

There are about 20 people involved with elements of the TDX supply chain, ranging from planning, purchasing, administration and warehouse teams.

But what happens when TDX doesn't have a particular item for a customer? “Whilst this can be frustrating for the customer, it is also when the TDX team spirit really comes through,” said James.

“We all get together to work out the best and fastest solution to solve a particular customer problem and get the parts into the country. We have established relationships with our supply partners, freight forwarders and domestic freight providers. The focus is on communicating with our Branch staff who deal directly with the customers.

“They know they can count on TDX to go the extra mile.”

New to the team, but already proving to be an invaluable asset


When loaders need to be cranking all the time and downtime is a dirty word, there's a new Field Service Technician in town who is going above and beyond to help keep TDX customers operating.

Hayden Tooley is a new Field Service Technician who has been with the TDX team since August last year and is already proving to be someone who lives and breathes what we're all about. He is part of the Christchurch team and looks after the Aoraki Region, specifically Ashburton and Timaru.

“Hayden has a great attitude, always has a grin on his face and has a great way of doing business. He is an absolute rockstar when it comes to looking after our customers in his area,” said Operations Manager for the Upper South Island region, Scott Moodie.

“He goes out of his way to be accommodating of our customers. Some of them have early starts and little opportunity for downtime, so he plans visits when it's most convenient for them. They really appreciate that he works around their schedule.”

Hayden covers a huge variety of different customer operations and applications, ranging from silage through to logging.

“Not only does he have a great work ethic and has developed a great understanding of Volvo product in a short amount of time, he understands customer needs and cares about what matters to them - he always keeps them front of mind,” added Scott.

“Hayden is always looking to improve on his product knowledge throughout the range of equipment that TDX offers to the market. He works in well with the Christchurch based Field Service Technicians and reaches out when he needs a hand, meaning we have a well-rounded and capable team member who excels at his work but knows his limitations – overall he's invaluable to the team.”

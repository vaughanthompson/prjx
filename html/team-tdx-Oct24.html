Introduction by Colm Hamrogue


Greetings team.

This has been a particularly challenging few weeks for many of us, especially those at the Hautapu Branch.

Personally, Zian's passing has been one of the most challenging moments in my career and I'm sure has affected many of you in a similar way.

I would like to acknowledge Zian and express, on the behalf of TDX and LDX, our condolences to his family and friends here and in South Africa.

I would also like to thank everyone who has supported the Hautapu team and been involved with the Worksafe and Police investigations.

Here at TDX, we are a family. We don't just work alongside each other, we celebrate each other's success, we also encourage each other when things aren't going right. We are a tight knit team and can lean on each other when we need help.

Please always know there are people you can speak with if you need to vent, to discuss a problem, ask for advice or just have a good old fashioned chat.

At TDX we provide a free, confidential independent service with professional counsellors to support all employees with life challenges and changes.

Your manager (and the People & Safety Team) have more information on how to access this programme as and when you feel you may need it.

Alternatively, you can book online or speak to someone 24/7 via the link here - www.ocp.co.nz/about

Sometimes it's hard to know what to do or what to say if you notice a friend or loved one struggling. There is a great resource called Life Keepers. It is a program that you can work your way through at your own pace - you can start and stop as you like, and in total it will take you about 2 hours to complete. It has some super helpful information on how to talk to someone who may be considering suicide or self-harm. Here is a link to this platform. www.lifekeepers.nz

Please, be safe out there both at home and work, look out for each other and never second guess stopping to ask someone if they're ok and if they'd like a chat.

Your actions could make a significant difference to someone's life.

Thank you.

New TDX team member delivering new business opportunities eight weeks in.


When someone starts a new job, it can sometimes take a bit of time to get settled into their role or get established in an organisation. This isn't the case with new TDX team member Daniel Hockley. He is the Shell Territory Manager based at Hautapu and, into only his second month, has already kicked some rather significant goals.


“Daniel joined TDX only eight weeks ago,” said Jason Steele, Business Manager for Shell Lubricants.

“He came from a technical sales role being a qualified precision tool maker by trade with an eye to detail and an inquisitive nature, so this fitted perfectly into the understanding of what we do in our consumable business at TDX.

“He's passionate, enthusiastic and is eager to start getting some runs on the board. And he's certainly done that, I'm really impressed with this big fish he's been able to land.”

The customer we're talking about is Modern Transport Engineers (MTE), a business which has been around since 1973 and which is made up of eight different companies building custom transport trailers, transport parts warehouse, equipment hire, fleet maintenance group and Sinotruk distributor.

Daniel was only into his second month when he had already identified them, contacted a key decision maker which led to a joint face to face meeting.

“Following this we had a couple of revisits to the customer drawing in all knowledge areas of our consumable business with John McElhinney working with Daniel on the customer offer and Jonathan Crocker focusing on their workshop pumping equipment requirements and overviewing our oil sampling program,” said Jason.

“Ultimately, we grew this on to the opportunity to present not just for their trailer manufacturing business - around 10,000L of oil - but we went in with a wider proposal for the whole group.

“Our proposal was built on the greater TDX offer and the different areas of our business that could work with them, be it small engines for their container lifting trailers, parts, and service for their fleet of aged Volvos and Yanmar's and of course new equipment sales opportunities.

“The customer was very impressed with our approach and competitive nature of our overall savings and ultimately came on board to supply their whole business in all things consumable - an account which had been held by Total.



“This is now a partnership with the potential of over 50,000L a year and Daniel is continuing to work closely with them to ensure we capture as much of the opportunity as possible.

“This customer is a good example where we approach from a wider-angle drawing in team members with different skill sets and experience as well as the wider approach of what TDX has to offer rather than just looking at the obvious. TDX has the potential to provide a range of value propositions to our customers and the knowledge, skills and passion to deliver on them. The work with Modern Transport Group is a perfect example of this and they are now becoming a very key customer for the development of our Cambridge business.

“Thanks to Daniel and everyone involved in getting this exciting opportunity across the line - it's an outstanding example of the TDX team working together to deliver an amazing result and has resulted in significant new business.”

 

The TDX Rebuild Centre


The TDX Rebuild Centre, located at Head Office in Christchurch, could be considered the place where old or tired machinery goes for a new lease of life. Rather than dispose of old or failing components, the Rebuild Centre works their magic and makes them as good as new before sending them back into the field for another round of service.


Mark Greenlow runs the Rebuild Centre, a purpose-built resource which supports the TDX network branches. It receives components from satellite branches then strips and asseeses them before making the decision to rebuild - or not. The rebuilt unit is then sent back to the branch before being used as a cost-effective repair or replacement option for a TDX customer.

The Rebuild department was split out as a standalone department eight years ago and has grown considerably in a few short years.

“In addition to myself, there's Scott our service advisor/parts wizard, four mechanics and an apprentice. It's a great crew which has been here for quite a while, the longest serving mechanic - Steve - has just ticked over 25 years with TDX.

“It's a really interesting job, there's a massive variety of machines we have the chance to work on and develop solutions for.

“For example, we've just finished rebuilding two 671 two-stroke Detroit diesel long blocks, destined to power a sugar train in Fiji. The engine was designed in the late 30's and has been out of production since 1995, the fact that they are still around today is a testament to how great this engine is. Actually, these two engines are replacing engines supplied 15-20 years ago.

“It demonstrates the relationship TDX has with the customer, especially our commitment to them as part of our brand values.

“One of the most satisfying things about our job is saving unnecessary waste and providing a new and cost effective option for our customers.

“In a nod to the original TransDiesel team, we remanufacture transmissions and put them into stock. They then become a viable, locally-available alternative to buying a brand new transmission, which often needs to be brought in from offshore at considerable time and expense.

“Our customers appreciate that we can now offer other options. As you could imagine, there is a massive price difference comparing new from factory, a service exchange part or a Rebuild Centre item. Shopping local with TDX Rebuild not only appeals on price, but also helps limit downtime.

“You could say the Rebuild Centre is Kiwi ingenuity at its finest.”

Hero Staff Member:
Mark Keatley,
TDX Christchurch


When it comes to hitting the big numbers, Mark Keatley has proven himself time and time again. In his role as the Digital and E-Commerce Manager, TDX consistently achieves over 500,000 Google ad and 500,000 Facebook and Instagram impressions each month. This work is only one part of his job, but has contributed to him being recognised in this month's Hero a Staff Member section, as told by Marketing Manager, Marc Warr.


Mark started with TDX way back in April 2006, he's been part of the team for over 18-years. He's seen a lot of changes in the team as well as wider TDX business, but he's been a constant and continues to be a remarkable asset for the organisation.

In addition to hitting online ad impressions out of the park, what does a Digital Marketing Manager do? Mark plays a crucial role in today's business landscape, driving online visibility, brand awareness and customer engagement. By strategically managing digital campaigns, he helps the business reach our target audience, increase website traffic and deliver leads for our teams. His expertise in analytics, content creation, Search Engine Optimisation and social media ensures that marketing efforts are both effective and measurable, fostering growth in an increasingly digital world.

One of the beauties of digital marketing is that it can be targeted directly to potential customers, doing away with 'scatter gun' approaches to advertising of days gone by. This means a huge amount of Mark's time goes into ensuring that our marketing campaigns are being seen by the right audience.

As mentioned at the start, he's hitting massive numbers on Google and social media, also engaging with 10,957 followers on our Facebook page as well as 376,000 active users who have visited the TDX website this year - that's more than twice the size of Hamilton or 3.5- times the size of Dunedin!

I also want to make mention of some of the highlights and projects that Mark has worked on. These include the introduction of the TradeDoor eCommerce platform onto the tdxltd.co.nz, where we are transacting over $700,000 worth of sales per month.




Mark also led the development of the new LDX and TDX websites and is also responsible for bringing to market a huge number of promotions for Shell, Volvo and Yanmar. The most recent Yanmar campaign delivered over 132 leads, which led to 39 sales.

Awesome work Mark, you're an example of someone who has learnt new skills and is embracing new technologies to help TDX be world-class in the digital world!

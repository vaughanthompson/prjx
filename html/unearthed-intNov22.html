The TDX Internal E-Newsletter. November 2022.


In this issue When a 6% drop is a good thing! • Setting Up for Success • High Five - Meet James Coleman • Data Delivers Customer Support

 

Hi there

This month's newsletter focuses on a number of positive changes happening at TDX; like improving the way we welcome employees to our business, embracing technology to predict future service work or how great teamwork and communication can help our customers clear their debt each month.

Each of these stories has a focus on change within the business to ensure better results. But it doesn't have to be big changes that make the difference. Sometimes it's the smaller steps that can have the biggest impact, like putting that extra focus on health and safety. It could be as simple as starting each meeting with a safety moment, taking extra time to be more aware of our surroundings or looking out for our mates as we go about our business.

Personally, I have some big changes ahead as I have made the decision to leave TDX and head home to the UK to look after my mother. While I don't leave the business until next month this is a change that I'm embracing and looking forward to. I know there are going to be lots of things I miss, there are also going to be many things to enjoy.

So, I leave with the key message that seeking out change and better ways of doing things should be something we all keep top-of-mind and that we lead with an open mind rather than closing off significant possibilities that might lie ahead.

Kevyn.

When a 6% drop is a good thing!

Most of the TDX team will know Susan Bilski our Credit Controller, who started nine days before the 2020 lock down, and had to learn the ropes from home.

What you might not know is that over the past two and a half years Susan has managed to reduce our 90+ day debt from 10% to just 4%, with the help of the AR Team. While Susan says this is a collective effort, it comes down to her determination and strong communication skills.

“When I started at TDX we had several key accounts that were overdue by up to six months, or more. Sometimes invoices hadn't been paid because service work wasn't fully completed, or we were waiting for a part, but often it was just a slow payer. The key issue was that the credit control team didn't really know why an invoice hadn't been paid, or if there were any underlying issues. Whatever the issue, bad debt isn't great for any business.

“I was determined we were going to bring the 90+ day debt down and the best way to do that is to talk to people, both members of the TDX team and our customers. I started having conversations with the internal staff throughout all the branches, to try and find out if there are any issues on why the overdue invoice may not be paid.

"This info has helped me to decide if, and how, to approach customers. I treat every customer as an individual. It's crucial to have good communication so you have all the information needed to help with decision making.

Now, we've got to the point where the internal staff advise me if they know there is an issue, or potential issue. I put notes on the account and that has made life so much easier. It really is a whole of business approach to solving issues.

“I really appreciate the way that knowledge is being shared internally about our customers and their situation. We are more connected as a business. I'm now confident when calling a customer about an overdue debt, that I've got the full picture. We can then have a trusted and honest conversation, on what works for the customer and TDX. It's a win win for everyone”

Setting Up for Success


We are always looking for ways to improve the employee experience at work. As our induction process is critical to setting up a new employee for success, we wanted to start there. The Induction Refresh Project Team, which includes Alle McGrath and Caitlin Mills, pulled together a project committee, with representation from across the business, to see what needed to change and what could be improved.

“We want to set new TDX employees up for success by making them feel supported and by giving them the tools, resources and knowledge they need to do their job. That includes making sure they have a good understanding of the whole business and how all the moving parts fit together,” said Caitlin.

“It's also important that wherever you are based in TDX, you get the same induction experience and that every new staff member takes the same journey and has the same knowledge to help them do their job well. To make sure that happens, we have created planning and preparation resources to support our Onboarding Managers.


"We are also providing content and information for new starters to work through independently when learning about the business, including a series of short videos that cover the work that goes on within key areas. People can work through the onboarding information at a time that makes sense for them, rather than being overloaded with information at the beginning of their TDX journey.

A great onboarding programme for an organisation as complex as ours should be spread across a year, rather than trying to cram huge amounts of information into a few weeks”.

The Project Committee will meet to review the updated process and resources in late October and the Project Team look forward to launching to the business once all the new onboarding content has been created.

Data Delivers Customer Support

This month Laura Velasquez will be joining TDX as Product Support Analyst. Why do we need one of those you might ask! Well, Laura's role will be to monitor and analyse all machine data so that we can intercept any product failures before they happen.

The right analysis allows us to be ahead of the curve when it comes to our customers machines. Laura will be responsible for managing ActiveCare and making sure our customers are getting ongoing management reports on their machines. It's all part of our drive to provide the best customer support in alignment with the Volvo, Yanmar and Sennebogen strategies.

Keep an eye out for Laura and welcome our latest recruit from the Dominican Republic!

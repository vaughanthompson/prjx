One year in - and what a year it has been!


Hello everybody,

One year in. It is a big challenge coming into a new business and learning the ropes. Meeting new staff, customers, supplier and partners. I want to say thanks to everyone for welcoming me, getting me up to speed and helping me learn.

When I was a customer of TDX I was impressed with the people, the service and attitude for getting it done for customers. My view has not changed. We have some of the best people in the industry. I really do love working here. I grew up in a small rural town in Ireland and my Mum and Dad had one truck. To lead an organisation like TDX now, is a massive honour and privilege for me.

I will ask you one thing. I am a huge believer in feedback, and I am always open to it. If you have ideas on how I can be better at my job leading TDX to get a better outcome for you, your fellow staff members, customers, suppliers or partners, please pick up the phone and call me. My mobile number is 0275600544.

We can only make things better together.

Moving on to the numbers.

Our financial results will be available soon, we don't have finalised numbers to share just yet, but the draft results are looking positive. We had the largest month ever in the history of the business in March.

When we look at machinery sales, in the first quarter of this year we are holding 16% market share for the total equipment market. This is the same percentage of market share we had for the same period last year. While the overall market is back 10%, we are holding our own. This is great work. Let's keep it up.

We don't know what the future holds, but we do know that we are capable of meeting any challenge here at TDX. Key to this is having the people, product, loyal customers and strong partners and suppliers. We have all of these at TDX. We need to keep delivering good customer service and together we can weather any storm.

As you know, on May 8, our new CFO, Victoria Sergel, will start at TDX. Victoria is well experienced in the construction industry and will fit right into our culture.

And after almost 40-years as the driving force of our finances and numbers, the legendary Financial Controller Lee Jenkins is retiring. I would like to thank Lee for her full commitment and dedication to making TDX what it is today. Beyond her core role she has looked after so many things that are required to make a business of our size simply exist. We will be having a good party for Lee in the coming months with plenty of red wine, but for now it's a massive thank you from all of us. Lee is not leaving us, she will be staying on as our Company Secretary and a shareholder.

Our gear is getting bigger. We will deliver the very first Volvo R100 Rigid Hauler Dump Trucks in New Zealand, these impressive machines will have a new home in Central Otago. Congratulations to the team involved. This is brilliant news and a lot of hard work has gone into this from TDX and Volvo.

Speaking about Volvo. The senior management team for our region is visiting the country in September and they will be out and about through the country getting a good look at TDX. I will let you know what is happening closer to the time.

Having the borders open again is great as we can have our partners and suppliers in the country. You always get a better outcome looking at the whites of people's eyes.

Again, thank you. TDX was born in the same year as I was, 1981. I am delighted I came to work for this company. It is a fun place to work. People have the right attitude. We make stuff happen. We are not a corporate giant. All I ask is that you focus on your colleagues, do your best and love what you do. It makes it easy when you do, and that is what we need to show our customers.

Here is to TDX and all the people that make us Number One in New Zealand!

Thank you,
Colm.

 

Electromobility to the fore at triennial ConExpo

Every three years North America's largest trade show is held in Las Vegas, and this year two TDX representatives had the opportunity to travel Stateside and attend the event.


Andrew Falloon (Regional Sales Manager Equipment Southern, Christchurch) and Adam Mottram (Finance and Leasing Business Manager, Christchurch), together with more than 139,000 construction and fluid power professionals from 133 countries, spent four days exploring the massive show.

“A key objective attending the event was to see options and opportunities in electromobility, an expanding range of Volvo products, new Yanmar track loaders and to learn some best practices from the North American construction industry,” said Andrew.

“From Adam's perspective, he was really interested in the finance, funding and leasing considerations associated with these new machines as they are typically 2.5-3 times as expensive as the equipment we're familiar with today.

“It's a great environment to reconnect with customers, suppliers and partners, in many instances actually meeting them for the first time after recent years of online Zooming and Teams calls.”

As for the show itself?
“In a word, it's vast,” said Andrew.

“There are more than 2,400 exhibitors so it's a real challenge to get around the place and see everything. We walked more than 30,000 steps on each of the days we were there.”

Highlights for the guys included the remote control EC250 and the new EC500, while they also enjoyed looking at the huge array of new, future-focussed electromobility options.

“It was really pleasing to see Volvo leading the charge in this area, it gives a real insight into what might be coming our way in years to come and also provides great peace of mind knowing we're partnering with such an innovative and world-leading OEM.”

From Auckland to Greymouth: A massive, million-dollar machine on the move to the mine


When you're moving a million-dollar piece of machinery, you want to ensure everything goes according to plan. Thankfully for Trevor Newport and the TDX Nelson/ Marlborough/ West Coast team, the recent delivery of the new Volvo EC950 to HCL Equipment on the West Coast went off without a hitch.

“This is the first machine of its type and size we've placed on the West Coast, we found the logistics of getting the equipment to the site the biggest challenge,” said Trevor.

“The new machine came in two truckloads from Auckland and was PD'd at our Greymouth base. We then moved it 10km to the hill for assembly, which took two technicians from Christchurch about five days. From there the machine was walked the 7km to the mine site, which was a day-long exercise due to the narrowness of the road and challenging environment. It's now in position and doing a great job for the operator.

“We're particularly proud of introducing a range of machines to HCL Equipment, but this one is rather special due to its size and specialist nature.

“It's been made possible thanks to, in the first instance, the Greymouth Branch going above and beyond over many years to develop a trusting relationship.

“They, with support from TDX Head Office, have always been proactive, front footing any issues and working hard to resolve them. It's these actions which build credibility and create opportunities such as this one with the EC950.”






Warrick Baxter from HCL Equipment echoed Trevor's words, reinforcing the strength of the relationship with TDX.


“The main driver behind the new machine was the relationship we enjoy with TDX. We have had issues with some trucks in the past, but the way TDX and Volvo worked to resolve these problems speaks volumes about the type of people we're dealing with.

“This provided us with confidence, which certainly contributed to our decision to choose the EC950.



“On these big production sites diggers are the tool of our trade, the lifeblood of the operation. In this business, if a machine goes down then it takes the rest of the site with it.

“Customer service is paramount, we need to know that if something goes wrong then the team will be here to help as quickly as possible.

“We need to have confidence in the product, but arguably of more importance is having trust in your partner. This is something we have in the team at TDX.”

Embracing change creating opportunity for TDX in Silverdale


For many people, change can be confronting - but this month's 'Hero Staff Member' is about one of our team who has embraced it and is helping to make a significant difference to TDX customers at our Silverdale branch.

“Daniel Teura, our Parts & Service Co-Ordinator at Silverdale, has taken what could have been a negative for our brand and turned it into a real win for TDX,” said Jason Steele, Business Manager - Shell Lubricants, Auckland.

“Four months ago we moved into new premises and Daniel did a phenomenal job setting up the new facility from scratch, demonstrating flexibility in a difficult environment. He's really taken ownership and stamped his mark on the operation in such an awesome way.






“His passion and enthusiasm has created a really positive vibe in the shop and this is reflected in the complimentary calls and interactions we have with customers, which has in turn converted into an increase in business.

“Above all else, it reflects on TDX and aligns with our values and principles, specifically focussing on our customers and providing a level of service and know-how.

“He has displayed resilience in what could have been a challenging situation, ultimately one which has enabled him to shine and enhance his reputation amongst TDX and our customers.

“He has a real spark in his eye and a new found spring in his step and epitomises what the ultimate TDX team member should be.”

Excellent effort, Daniel - keep up the outstanding work!

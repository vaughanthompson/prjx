Introduction. Weathering the Storm: An Update from the Equipment Division. By Joe Gradwell.



For this month's newsletter, we caught up with Joe Gradwell to get an inside perspective on how the Equipment Division has navigated one of the most challenging market conditions in recent years and why there is growing optimism for what lies ahead.

“There is no sugar-coating it, the past 12-months have been tough. Like many across the industry, the Equipment Division has faced a subdued market, with customer confidence slower to return than many had hoped. The sales team's activity has remained high, with a strong level of quoting and customer engagement continuing across the market. The team has been working hard to progress opportunities, converting these into firm orders has remained challenging due to ongoing market conditions and strong competitive pressure across a number of deals,” Joes says.

"We've started to see some lift in the last few months, and we're beginning to see the hard work pay off.”

That success, he stresses, is the result of genuine hard work and a team effort.

“I'd like to give a shout out to the Equipment Team because these results are down to the hard work they've put in during some really trying times.”


Importantly, success hasn't happened in isolation. Joe says the ability to continue delivering for customers has depended on close collaboration across the entire business.

“We can't deliver equipment without the workshop doing their part, and weekly cross-department conversations have been critical in ensuring we meet our commitments. We've shifted resources around the country when needed and worked together to get the best outcomes for customers.”

Joe also paid tribute to the Aftersales team, who have gone above and beyond to help meet customer demand.

“It is fairly simple - if we can't deliver machines we simply don't get paid, in a tough market being agile and delivering is critical.”

From a product perspective, there is plenty to be excited about. Volvo's electrification drive is gathering momentum, with larger electric machine platforms expected to broaden market opportunities. Over the next 12 to 24-months, more than 80 new models are expected to come to market.

At the same time, the transition to FUCHS continues to gain momentum, with strong customer feedback around technology, operator comfort, safety and fuel efficiency. Recent successes with C3 Port operations are a clear example of the brand's growing traction in the market.

Meanwhile, TDX's Yanmar range is also receiving strong feedback, highlighted recently during a successful customer launch event in Auckland.

“I'd like to say a huge thank you to Richard and the Yanmar team across the country, Yanmar is a huge part of what we do and it blows me away how many of these machines we manage to put into the market every single year,” said Joe.

For Joe personally, moving into the Sales side of the business has made for an exciting period.

“I'm incredibly grateful for the team we have across the country. They are experienced, hardworking people who continue to step up, even when conditions have been tough. I strongly feel that we have the best team within our industry.”

While difficult decisions, including cost reductions and restructuring, have been necessary during the downturn, Joe says the willingness of people across TDX to step in and help has been invaluable. The entire Equipment division team has stepped into support each other across the board.

“Hopefully we're through the worst of it. We've made great strides to weather the storm, and we're well positioned to seize opportunities as they come along.

“For example, we have just won a very significant deal with Fulton Hogan which will see new equipment head to FH sites in the regions. Much of this success is due to the outstanding ongoing efforts of Andrew Falloon and Richard Papps and their involvement with this customer, as this is the second time we have won a large order with FH. It illustrates the importance of having a positive and ongoing relationship with key accounts and ensuring they are looked after at all steps along the way at all of their sites around the country.

“We were not the cheapest option, but FH felt we were the best. This really comes down to us going above and beyond when it really counts, which is a solid team effort right across the board. So thank you to everyone for your collective efforts, they are genuinely appreciated.

“Pleasingly, the year has started very well and all indications are that it could be a very strong one for TDX. We need to keep our team and customers front of mind and support everyone to the best of our abilities, but I have high confidence that this could be a turning point for our business.”

 

Understanding Tyrewise: What It Means for Our Industry


You may have started hearing more about Tyrewise and the changes it brings to the New Zealand market. While the programme has been in discussion, consultation, and development for some time, it is now becoming a reality for industries that rely heavily on tyres. This includes the construction and heavy equipment sector.

So, what exactly is Tyrewise, and what does it mean for us and our customers?

What is Tyrewise?

Tyrewise is New Zealand's Government-backed regulated product stewardship scheme for end-of-life tyres. In simple terms, it is designed to ensure tyres are responsibly collected, managed, and recycled once they have reached the end of their useful life, rather than ending up in landfill or being stockpiled.

The initiative applies across all sectors that import or sell tyres, including construction, transport, agriculture, and materials handling. To support the scheme, a levy is now placed on every invoice of a tyred machine, helping fund the safe disposal and recycling of tyres at the end of their life.

How Does It Work?

Tyrewise is funded through a stewardship fee applied when tyres enter the New Zealand market, whether imported directly or fitted to new machinery.

For our industry, this means a fee will apply to every new tyre, including those supplied as part of a new machine or sold as a replacement tyre.

That funding is then used to support:

  • Collection and transport of used tyres
  • Recycling and processing initiatives
  • Development of sustainable end markets for tyre-derived products

When a tyre reaches the end of its life, it can be returned through approved Tyrewise channels, with disposal costs already accounted for through the scheme.

What This Means for the Construction Equipment Sector

There are several practical implications for our customers and operations.

Greater transparency in costs

The Tyrewise levy will become a visible part of tyre pricing. While it introduces an additional cost component, it ensures the true cost of disposal is accounted for upfront, rather than deferred or unmanaged.

A more sustainable industry

This is a positive step toward reducing environmental impact across construction and heavy equipment operations. By supporting responsible tyre recovery and recycling, the sector can play a stronger role in sustainable practices.

Operational considerations

Customers will need to ensure end-of-life tyres are returned through approved Tyrewise collection channels to ensure compliance with the programme.

Changes for equipment supply and aftermarket support

For OEM suppliers and dealers like us, the levy will apply to both machine sales where tyres are included and aftermarket tyre supply. Pricing structures and quotes will need to clearly reflect the fee, and we expect increased customer questions around how it is applied.

Our Role

As a key supplier to the construction and heavy equipment sector, our role is to educate customers on what Tyrewise is and why it exists, ensure transparency around the fee, and provide practical support where needed.

Tyrewise represents a significant shift in how tyres are managed in New Zealand. While change always brings questions, the long-term outcome is a more sustainable, compliant, and responsible approach to tyre disposal across our industry.

If you have questions or receive customer enquiries about Tyrewise, please reach out — we'll continue sharing updates as the scheme evolves.

 

TDX and C3: A Growing Success Story Powered by FUCHS


Strong partnerships are built on trust, performance and results — and the growing relationship between TDX and C3 is a great example of all three in action.


C3, a major player in on-wharf logistics and port operations across New Zealand, first starting talking with TDX and FUCHS in mid-2025.

After working closely to understand the demands of C3's operation, the first machine was placed into service in February 2026 - a FUCHS MHL360 Material Handler.

The feedback since then has been overwhelmingly positive.

Operating in busy port environments where productivity, reliability and operator performance are critical, C3 has been extremely impressed with the quality and capability of the MHL360.

In particular, operators have praised the outstanding visibility offered from the machine, allowing them to work with confidence and precision in demanding conditions.

Comfort has also stood out as a major advantage. The high level of operator comfort, combined with the thoughtful and practical cabin layout, has made a significant difference for those spending long hours in the machine.

Another key point of difference has been fuel efficiency. Compared with other brands in C3's fleet, the MHL360 has delivered exceptional fuel performance — an increasingly important factor in today's operating environment where efficiency and cost management matter more than ever.

The strongest endorsement, however, comes in the form of action.

C3 has been so impressed with the performance of its first FUCHS machine that it has recently placed an order for a second MHL360. In fact, feedback from the customer suggests that if budget was no barrier, they would be eager to replace their entire fleet with FUCHS equipment.

For TDX, it is a fantastic example of how understanding customer needs, delivering the right equipment and providing strong ongoing support can turn a new relationship into a genuine success story.

John McElhinney: Experience, Expertise and a Passion for Getting Things Done


After more than 13 years with TDX, John McElhinney has built a reputation as someone who can always be relied upon to make things happen.


A Key Account Manager for the Shell business, John is the senior member of the team and recently celebrated a milestone birthday. But while the years of experience are considerable, colleagues say he is still a ‘spring chicken at heart.' He is someone who is always eager to get involved, tackle challenges and keep things moving.

A diesel mechanic by trade, John brings an impressive depth of technical knowledge to the role, paired with a sharp eye for detail and an unwavering commitment to delivering a world-class product and customer experience.

John's TDX journey began as Product Manager for the ENI lubricants, he then moved into the role of North Island Sales Manager before successfully transitioning through the move from ENI to Shell and becoming Shell Key Account Manager. His role covers the guardianship of 20 key customers which represents around 45% of our current Shell sales.

Managing accounts of that scale means navigating plenty of moving parts, but John's deep understanding of both TDX and its customers ensures issues are solved quickly and relationships remain strong.

In today's environment, that expertise matters more than ever. Ongoing disruption in the Middle East has created challenges for lubricant supply chains across the industry, requiring transparent communication and some tough conversations with customers. John's calm, pragmatic approach and commitment to keeping customers informed have been invaluable.

“John's work ethic takes a significant load off my plate, giving me confidence and peace of mind that things will be handled properly” says Jason Steele, GM Shell and Aftersales Support.

Humble, passionate and refreshingly down to earth, John also plays an important mentoring role within the team. He is always willing to share his knowledge, encouraging others to learn through doing and leading by example every day.


Simply put, John is an example of what good looks like.

Turning a Problem into Progress: Napier Team Develop Innovative Safety Solution


At TDX Napier, the team has been proving exactly what it means to live the philosophy of 'Say something, See something, do something.'


After noticing a recurring issue around back injuries linked to one particular piece of machinery and specific maintenance tasks, the Napier team decided it was time to do more than simply manage the problem — they wanted to solve it.

The injuries were often occurring during a difficult lifting process involving heavy equipment inside a machine cab. Recognising the risk and the physical strain involved, the team came together to explore a safer, smarter way forward.

Working collaboratively across the site, the Napier team began developing an innovative prototype aimed at removing the manual lifting component entirely.

The result?

A specially designed lifting device that bolts directly into the cab, transforming what had previously been a difficult and physically demanding manoeuvre into an easy lifting solution. Most importantly, it significantly reduces the need for people to manually handle heavy equipment, helping remove the risk of injury before it happens.

It's a fantastic example of a team identifying an ongoing challenge and taking ownership to create meaningful change. Rather than accepting a recurring issue as 'just part of the job,' the Napier team went above and beyond to develop a practical solution with the potential to make a real difference.

While the device is still in the prototype and development phase, and requires official signoff from a certified engineer before any wider implementation, the progress made so far has been incredibly impressive.

If successful, the innovation could deliver benefits well beyond Napier, with the potential to be rolled out across TDX branches nationwide and support safer maintenance practices throughout the business.

A huge acknowledgement to the Napier team for their initiative, collaboration, and commitment to making TDX a safer place to work. Great ideas often start with someone speaking up and this is proof of what can happen when we take action.

Well done to the Napier team, great work!

Hero Staff Member - Stu Drew


Welcome to TDX: Introducing Stu Drew. This month, we're pleased to introduce a new face to the TDX team - Stu Drew, who joins us as Central/Lower North Island Operations Manager, based in Napier.


Although Stu has been with TDX for less than two months, he arrives with an impressive track record and brings a wealth of experience that makes him a standout addition to the business. With more than 22 years of senior leadership experience across machinery sales, rentals, construction, agriculture and the hire industry, Stu adds significant expertise to our growing team.

Most recently, Stu held the role of Vice President Operations - New Zealand at Brandt, and his career has also included leadership positions with AB Equipment and Hirepool, following its merger with Hirequip. Across these organisations, Stu has developed deep operational and sales knowledge while leading teams across large geographic regions, managing budgets, planning and delivering strong outcomes.

Beyond his technical and operational expertise, Stu is passionate about people and leadership. He believes success comes from a shared vision and empowering teams to achieve great things together. With strengths in mentoring, coaching and team building, Stu is committed to creating environments where people feel valued, supported and challenged to succeed.

Stu's extensive background also brings fresh thinking to TDX. His experience identifying operational improvements and solving complex business challenges will help strengthen support for staff across the regions he oversees, while opening new opportunities for growth and development.

Importantly, Stu's appointment reflects TDX's ability to attract high-calibre talent, reinforcing that TDX is a desirable place to work and a business committed to investing in people who can help us continue to grow from strength to strength.

Please join us in welcoming Stu to the TDX team. We look forward to the experience, leadership and opportunities he will bring as we continue building regional success together.

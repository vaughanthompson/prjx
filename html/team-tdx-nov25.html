Introduction by Greg Slaughter, CEO.



Hi team,

It's great to be opening this month's newsletter off the back of such a strong October. The financial results across the board were excellent — a real credit to your efforts, focus, and teamwork. As we look ahead to the end of the year, it's a timely reminder to keep chasing every opportunity and to remain disciplined around cost control. Every bit counts in this competitive market.

I've really enjoyed getting out and about recently — including some valuable time on the road visiting some key customers including Frost Fans, C3, QUBE and Fulton Hogan in Mt Wellington — and I've had the pleasure of spending time in a number of our branches, Napier, Auckland and Hautapu.

There's a great feeling of energy and momentum out there, and a growing sense of positivity returning to the market, which is great to see. I also appreciate how tough and competitive things remain — your resilience and commitment continue to make the difference.

This month's edition is full of great examples of that. From the clever thinking in Taupo, where the team continues to punch above its weight, to the innovation shown by Jason Brashier and the crew developing a new Dafo install template — it's smart, practical solutions like this that are making a real impact for our customers.

Mark Greenlow and the Ops team also demonstrated how safety and efficiency can go hand in hand, with the rollout of Loadrite scales on our forklifts helping reduce risk and improve accuracy.

And of course, a special shoutout to Jonathan Crocker — our very own “Oil Doctor” — whose technical knowledge and customer-first approach has delivered exceptional results, including helping a customer save over $100,000 a year. That's the kind of value that sets us apart in the market.

At the end of this month, Alister and I will be heading to Europe to spend quality time with our key OEM partners including Volvo, Kohler, Perkins and FUCHS. We will be spending time discussing our business plans for the coming year and continuing to strengthen our relationships.

As we head into the “silly season” of Christmas — one of the busiest periods of the year — I want to thank each of you for everything you've contributed this year. Let's keep the momentum going, look after one another, and finish the year strong.

Take care and talk soon,
Greg

 

Loadrite Scales Take the Guesswork Out of Lifting


The TDX team recently discovered just how accurate — and valuable — the new Loadrite weight scales are on their Linde forklift. While calibrating the system, the team decided to put it to the test by lifting a Volvo L250 transmission.


Service Manager - Rebuild/Allison, Mark Greenlow, recalls:

“We picked up the transmission and the Loadrite scales showed the weight instantly. We asked one of our techs to take a guess, and he figured it was around 800kg — but it turned out to be 1.2 tonnes! These scales take the guesswork out of lifting.”

For a team that regularly handles heavy equipment, accuracy matters. With the Loadrite scales, operators can now confirm load weights on the spot, ensuring every lift is carried out safely and efficiently. Previously, items had to be moved and weighed at the warehouse, adding time and risk to the process.

The benefits go beyond convenience — they're a big step forward for safety. As Phillip Bracefield, GM Operations, explains:

“We plan to roll out Loadrite scales across our forklifts as they come up for replacement in the TDX fleet over the next 18 months. We will be retro fitting the Loadrite system to the forklifts that were replaced last year shortly also. It's about keeping our people safe. The Loadrite system is another great example of how we live our health and safety value of Take Care — making sure we look after our people every day.”

The rollout represents another innovation supporting TDX's commitment to safety, precision, and continuous improvement in operations.
 

Maximising Uptime with a Smart New Template


Sometimes, the best innovations come from the workshop floor — and this one's a great example of how teamwork and a bit of forward thinking can deliver big wins for both TDX and our customers.

During a recent Dafo fire suppression install on an EW240 Volvo Material Handler (with raised cab), Jason Brashier our Product Support Technician spotted a unique opportunity.

Knowing we had several of these installs to do he realised that by developing a template for the Dafo system install, we could streamline the installation and commissioning process for future jobs.

What started as an idea during a predelivery check quickly turned into an innovative, time-saving solution.

“It's all about maximising uptime for the customer,” says Phil Smith.

“We developed a cost-effective kit and template that can now be used on these specialised spec for purpose machines. It can take us 40 hours to do the job — now with the template in place we were able to smash it out in one day with two guys.”

The results speak for themselves. At the Enviro Waste Sunshine Ave site, the team completed the install over a weekend while the machine was not in use.

“The customer was absolutely stoked,” says Phil.

“They came back to work on Monday, and it was business as usual — no downtime, no disruption.”

There are already another three machines in the pipeline to complete and the savings in both time and cost will be significant. What once required two to three days of machine downtime now only takes one day.

The success of this project highlights how good prepwork and collaboration can drive great outcomes.

“It really was a team effort,” Phil says.

“Two guys handled the prework, Jason Brashier and Kavi Narayan and another two did the install and commissioning thanks to John Young and Desmond Davidson. We had great support from the wider TDX group, including our parts teams. That's what makes these innovations possible.”

TDX Taupo - Teamwork and Timber Talk


If you've ever driven into Taupo, you've probably spotted the impressive TDX branch standing proudly by the highway, you really can't miss it.


The modern, well-equipped facility, complete with a five-tonne overhead crane, large warehouse and sharp-looking showroom makes quite the statement. But it's not just the building that stands out — it's the people inside it.

With a team of ten, including three technicians, an apprentice and some serious experience under the roof, Taupo punches well above its weight. “The team here are incredibly skilled,” says Andrew Ballsillie, who joined TDX earlier this year after 36 years as a customer.

“They've built great relationships with customers and they consistently make things happen time and time again.”

That skill and teamwork extend beyond Taupo's walls. The branch is part of the Central North Island region, alongside Rotorua, Gisborne, and Tauranga, and the collaboration between the four is something Andrew says really stands out. “It's a very supportive network. When one branch is quiet, another steps in to help out. It's a great example of how teamwork across branches delivers results.”

The Taupo crew are well-known in forestry circles, with most of their work coming from local logging crews handling machines like the Volvo L90,L120 or EC300. And when it comes to technical know-how, there's no shortage of talent.

Longtime TDX stalwart Graeme Nell has been with the company for over a decade, while Ewen Satherly, the Territory Manager, is the go-to guy for all things forestry — “if Ewen doesn't know about it, it probably hasn't been invented yet,” laughs Andrew.

Add in Taupo's proud record in the Volvo Masters competition, and it's clear this team's reputation is well earned. They are a strong, supportive, and skilled crew keeping the Central North Island moving.

Hero Staff Member - The Oil Doctor: Spotlight on Jonathan Crocker


When it comes to lubricants keeping machines running smoothly, few people know their stuff like Jonathan Crocker, TDX Technical Manager based in Christchurch.

With over 20 years at TDX under his belt, Jonathan's reputation for technical expertise, customer care, and good old-fashioned problem-solving has made him a bit of a legend, both inside the business and out in the field.

“Johnny brings real value to technical discussions in front of customers,” says Jason Steele, who manages the team.

“He helps customers make smart, informed decisions, particularly around lubricants. One customer told me Johnny's advice on grease selection, didn't just help the business; he kept me in business. By simply identifying the correct product to use saved the customer over $100,000 a year in costly repairs which he had previously just thought was normal for the application. That's the level of impact he has.”

One of Jonathan's specialties is oil sampling or, as Jason puts it, “It's like a doctor analysing a blood test.”

By running used oil analysis and interpreting lab data, Johnny can identify potential issues before they become costly breakdowns. With over than 25,000 samples passing through the TDX Status Oil Analysis program each year, Johnny has some great stories of the savings made by early detection and actions.

“He figures out what's wrong, explains it clearly, and helps customers fix it,” says Jason. “That's real, practical value.”

Johnny's expertise isn't just about machines, it's about trust, credibility, and reliability. “The work JC does in front of customers shows what good looks like,” Jason says. “He's a key man in our Shell team.”

And speaking of Shell, the team's efforts are paying off.

“There are more than 150 lubricant brands in our market, and Shell isn't the cheapest, everything we do has to add value,” Jason explains.

“But we've seen some fantastic results. October was our best monthly volume in two years, and Shell recognised TDX for the best growth in Oceania. That's a huge achievement and a team effort, from sales to delivery. I'm hugely grateful to everyone involved.”

With experts like Johnny leading the way, it's clear TDX's winning formula runs as smoothly as the machines they support.

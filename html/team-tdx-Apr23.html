TDX team kicking big goals as Coasters embrace relationship-based business partnerships

An expanded TDX presence on the West Coast is helping Dan Watson and his team achieve some impressive results amongst clients in the mining community.


"The West Coast is a place where big gear is needed to get big things done, it's a world where 40-tonne and above dump trucks and excavators rule the roost," said Dan Watson - Service Manager, West Coast Branch.

Over the past four years, we've been able to grow our presence and develop partnerships with customers. This has proven invaluable as it helps establish trust and build credibility, so much so that TDX is considered the new benchmark on the Coast.

"The TDX team deals with million-dollar-plus kit day in and day out, so given this level of investment we need to be one step ahead of our partners when it comes to anticipating their needs and requirements."

In conjunction with growing the team from two to eight employees, in May last year TDX moved into a bigger site with greater frontage. This increased visibility has been a real contributor to positive change.

"An increased presence provides customers with peace of mind, they need to feel confident dealing with us and know we're here to listen and support them. It's so much more than a business transaction, it's all about developing a trusting and reliable partnership."

Of particular satisfaction for Dan and the team is knowing there is a big, happy and growing customer base across the West Coast.

"Much like the original TDX story, we started from small and humble beginnings and have grown into one of the premier workshops on the Coast. The rapid expansion of the business has resulted in 'a few sleepless nights and a few more grey hairs', but it's also incredibly exciting to see the growth and satisfying to know we're making a real difference to our customers."

“We've grown our presence, increased the number of techs, enjoy greater and much-appreciated support from Product support and Sales team and have become the benchmark amongst the heavy machinery business,” said Dan.

The new branch has dramatically improved the working environment for the TDX team. Having moved from a skyline garage to a purpose-built workshop with three full-length work bays and a separate part warehouse the team is more organised and efficient in their work.

“Working as a cohesive team with great communication, we've been able to provide a world-class service for our customers and kick some massive goals.”

Dan and the team have some big news to share in the coming weeks, as they're in the process of delivering a new EC950 to HCL at ROA mine.

“At the rate, we're going, we'll be needing a bigger building next year.”

'Locals Helping Locals' making a difference in the Deep South


The new 'Locals Helping Locals' initiative is a core principle of our business, as it helps foster genuine relationships between TDX and local communities around the country.

“Each TDX branch, via their Operations Manager, is allocated a budget to support local organisations, events or environmental projects,” said Marc Warr, Marketing Manager.

“This financial assistance is a tangible way that TDX is giving back to local communities, we're putting our money where our mouth is and demonstrating true examples of 'locals helping locals.'”

One branch which has embarked on its TDX community partnership journey is the team in Invercargill, led by Daryl Harper - Operations Manager, Lower South Island.

“We had a discussion amongst the staff and decided to support the Invercargill Loss and Grief centre. Most of us hadn't heard of them before, but after visiting their operation and learning about the services they provide, we're really proud to be assisting them and covering some of their operational expenses - funding which quite literally helps to keep the lights on,” said Daryl.

“There are some other more prominent charities and organisations down our way which receive significantly more monetary donations, but in this case, it's not the size of the contribution, it's the intent behind it.




“In the case of the Loss and Grief Centre, it's a service open to all members of the community, from youngsters through to adults, it's available to anyone who needs someone to talk with or some form of support.

“It's a meaningful donation for all involved, especially as any one of us might have a need to utilise their services at some point in our lives. That's why it's such a great organisation to be involved with.

“I'm looking forward to seeing which groups other TDX branches choose to support... It's often the little groups which operate on minimal funding and are out of the spotlight which provides the greatest good across the widest spread of the community.”

More information about the Invercargill Loss and Grief Centre, together with the services they provide, can be found on their website.

New TDX trailer setting the standard in mobile service functionality


The latest addition to the TDX fleet is a brand new, fully-branded mobile Service Trailer, one which sets a new level of professionalism as well as enhanced on-site functionality.

“The old service trailer was well overdue for replacement,” said Scott Moodie, Operations Manager - Upper South Island.

“The team recognised that we needed a trailer that was high-quality and fit for purpose. It was important that the trailer could do the job, but also reflects the great machines we sell to our customers; we want them to know they're going to have a world-class experience dealing with TDX.

We have gone for a braked Williams trailer, the higher-spec delivers safety and longevity, as well as providing a great all-round balance when towing. The pumping and drum portion of the trailer has been manufactured by Advanced Fluid Control systems, which are New Zealand's leading supplier and installer of specialist fluid transfer equipment, giving us the best of both worlds in this mobile fluid transfer system.

“The new trailer also ticks the box from a safety and environmental standpoint, as the unit is fully self-contained - there is no risk of oil dropping between the unit being serviced and the trailer itself - which also removes the possibility of weather contamination in the oil. And, for added peace of mind, we have added spill kits to the trailer.”

According to Kieran Lindsay, TDX Christchurch Service Manager, the new trailer offers significantly enhanced operational capabilities.

“The new unit is the first of its sort in the TDX fleet of service operation vehicles, it boasts gull-wing style lift upside doors which help make access to the three-drum pump system incredibly easy and straightforward,” said Kieran.




“It's incredibly well organised in terms of where everything is located, ranging from placement and stowage of retractable reels through to digital dials to measure the amount of oil used at a particular service.

“It's also easy to manoeuvre into tight spots and difficult situations. From an operator's perspective, having your service machine beside you provides a huge benefit, as it does away with trudging between the work site and wherever you have parked your vehicle.

“It really is the ultimate when it comes to a practical solution that addresses customer needs and makes it easier and quicker for our team to cater for customer requirements.”

Hero Staff Member: Phil Oakley - TDX Service Manager, Whangarei and Silverdale.


'Doing the basics well' delivering big results for Phil Oakley and the TDX team in Whangarei

Whangarei is a critical region for TDX, with a number of key customers and accounts. Developments of the past year have seen our most northern-based crew deliver some real wins.

“Whangarei has always been a really competitive region and one that we had to get right when it came to getting the right people on our team. People who would deliver a great customer experience for our customers,” said Joe Gradwell, General Manager Aftersales.

“The team led by Phil Oakley has developed a strong team culture, which is resulting in an increasing number of happy customers. Ultimately the team are doing the basics right.

“Communication is key, both within the team and in keeping customers up to date and informed. Phil has taken the lead on building proactive and regular contact with some of our national and local customers operating in the region such as Winstones Aggregates, Forest Loaders and C3” said Joe.

“This attention to detail and level of service has delivered increased business. There have been increased levels of positive customer feedback about the team.. Word-of-mouth is the best form of advertising, when you do a good job everyone wants to deal with you and it has resulted in big growth. It's having a positive rub-off on the team with demand for their services increasing constantly.”

Phil and the team demonstrate TDX values every day, specifically keeping the customer front of mind, providing timely and reliable service, going above and beyond and providing local know-how. Phil has really stepped up, is leading by example and is ensuring nothing slips through the cracks,” said Joe.

“I'd like to personally thank Phil and the team for the outstanding work he is doing, both with our customers but also developing and leading the team, the results speak for themselves.”

Well done, team!

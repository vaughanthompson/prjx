Hi everyone

 

This year has flown past with December already here. Before we know it, it will be 2023.

In October, as you will know from my videos, I was one of over 495,000 people from more than 200 countries that visited Bauma 22 in Munich. Bauma is the world's leading trade fair for construction machinery, building material machines, mining machines, construction vehicles and construction equipment. It was fantastic to spend time with so many industry representatives who share our passion for all things heavy machinery.

This contact included valuable time with some of our key partners including Melker Jernberg, President of Volvo Construction Equipment in Sweden, Erich Sennebogen one of the family owners of Sennebogen and Giuliano Parodi, Global CEO at Yanmar Compact Equipment and Chairman of the Board at Yanmar Construction Equipment in Germany. What struck me as key to these interactions was the fact that it didn't matter which company these contacts represented, what mattered was the personal connections made and the opportunity to have good, open and honest dialogue that brought us closer together.

While we sell heavy equipment, lubricants, service, parts and power generators, what our business really relies on for success is strong and enduring relationships and connections. These can only be built on honesty, transparency and respect. And it doesn't matter whether you're talking to a Global CEO, a smaller supplier, a customer or a team member, if you don't have these essential qualities, then the rest won't follow, and it certainly won't stick. At the end of the day, we are quite simply a people business with strong relationships at our core.

This is the reason that we are investing in training to give our teams the skills to be expert at managing customer centric relationships. The Customer Centric Teams Training will involve a range of people from all different divisions across our business. Customer centric training puts customer interaction at the centre of everything we do to ensure that the experience our customers receive is consistent, focused and evolving to meet their needs. We intend on rolling this training out to all staff in time, with the second round planned for February 2023.

Thank you for feedback sent from you to me. The insights you choose to share, help to hold me to account and help to make sure that I keep our customers at the forefront of my thinking. You have shared valuable feedback about what we can all do to make TDX a better place for all our connections to flourish. Many of your ideas will be put in place.

I hope that you all enjoy a restful break over the Christmas period, and I look forward to working alongside you all in 2023.

Best wishes,
Colm.

Meeting Melker Jernberg, President of Volvo Construction Equipment.

Talking
Turkey


Last month Sean Cole and Andrew Smith, from the Power Products team, visited the Teksan factory in Istanbul, Turkey. Teksan supply our TX generator range which provides TDX customers with an affordable and reliable option for standby power generation.

“It was great to have the opportunity to meet the Teksan team face to face. We started importing their product 18 months ago on a trial basis and it is an extremely reliable and popular option with TDX customers. Teksan is very similar to TDX in their operations and history. They are a privately owned, family company with their operating style very aligned with ours.

Three of the original founding family are still running the business. We have been meeting Teksan virtually since our relationship began, but nothing can match personal contact. While Teksan were responsive prior to the trip, we are now finding that the personal relationship has really added value to the way we interact. There's definitely an instant response when we call, and the discussions are much more relaxed and friendly. We now have a shared understanding of each other's needs and that makes it so much easier to do business. The TDX team should expect to notice business with Teksan really improving.



“Following the factory visit we joined a Teksan international distributor meeting in Antalya, a resort city on the Turkish Coast. This gave us the chance to talk with other Teksan customers and gain a shared understanding of product capability. We have brought back a lot of knowledge that will be shared with the TDX team and customers, but by far the most value will be gained from the ability to pick up the phone and have a more personal connection”.

Technology Review -Enterprise Resource Planning


The technology we use at TDX makes our business run smoothly and should make it easier for our team to do their jobs. But like all things, technology doesn't stand still. That's why the IT team have started a major review of the technology we use within TDX with a focus on our Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) platform.

Companies use ERPs to manage key operational aspects like financial transactions, stock management, service management and point of sale. Over the years at TDX we have modified our legacy software to meet business needs, but we have got to the point where these modifications are no longer up to scratch, and they are slowing the business down.

Chief Information Officer, Mick Skelton, says the technology review is really a business transformation project with technology as the enabler. It's not just a review of IT. So, the starting point for the project is a review of all TDX business processes, and supporting software, used across the business to gain an understanding of what works well and why, and what no longer meets business needs.

“This comprehensive review will help us understand what the IT team need to deliver as part of the project. Following this we can build a roadmap for delivery including timeframes and cost. We plan to have the review and roadmap side of things completed by February next year and from there we can start to understand what the business really needs to enable our teams to do their jobs well. It should all come together to make doing business easier for everyone - TDX staff and customers.

“Following the comprehensive review process, we might find that the business ends up with several best of breed solutions, with good integration, rather than an off the shelf or standard turnkey solution. We won't know the end result until we have completed the current investigation phase. What I can say with certainty is that this review is about making life easier for staff to do their jobs. It should be an enabler for our business, not something that slows people down.

So, if you get invited to participate in the technology review process please engage, and you can look forward to the roll out of the solution starting 1 April next year”.

The implementation phase will take up to take some time to complete but it will be transformational for TDX.

EVENTS, EVENTS, EVENTS...


November has been a very busy month for the marketing and sales teams completing three events

We attended the Fast Forward Forestry Expo in Rotorua, which was the first forestry supplier industry event that had happened in many years. It was a great opportunity to showcase our Volvo equipment, Allison transmissions, Dafo fire suppression and Rotobec grapples.

The event attracted over 5,000 industry visitors over the two days and the charity auction on the Friday night raised over $40,000 to support “Be a Mate” in Forestry, with one of our Volvo diecast models fetching over $700.

Next was the Trucking Industry Expo in Christchurch, where we showcased our Shell oil solutions, including having our oil truck on display. Our site was very busy with a truck racing simulator, bringing plenty of people in. The event was also attended by our very own Ask Rob to answer any technical questions and Brooke Fraser who partook in the careers drive element of the show.

Finally, the biggest event to pull together was Fieldays held in Mystery Creek in Hamilton. We had a huge site and showcased our Volvo and Yanmar range along with Dafo and Shell oil. We set up a very engaging site including a digger golf activation, 2 simulators, lounge and coffee area and of course our amazing sales team! We are running some show specials and expect to achieve some good sales and leads at the event.

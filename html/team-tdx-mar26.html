Introduction by John O'Callaghan, CFO.



Hi Team,

I wanted to share a quick update as we head toward the end of the financial year. It's been a busy period across the business, and I've appreciated the chance to get out to our sites and meet more of you as I settle into the CFO role.

Overall, our financial performance this period remains solid, and I'm encouraged by the resilience of the business and the disciplined approach we continue to take around cost management. Revenue is broadly tracking in line with expectations, supported by steady demand across our core markets. While a few areas have experienced softer activity, these have been balanced by stronger-than-expected results in others.

On the cost side, our continued focus on operational efficiency is helping us maintain margins despite ongoing inflationary pressure. Cashflow remains healthy, and our balance sheet is in a strong position to support the investments and strategic initiatives that will drive our next phase of growth.

Looking ahead, I'm confident in our trajectory. Our pipeline is strong, and we are well-positioned to capture opportunities in the coming quarters while managing risks proactively. A final push to wrap up WIP, get invoices out promptly, and follow up on overdue payments will help us finish the year well and set us up for a strong start next year.

We've also welcomed some new faces into the Finance team, and they've hit the ground running—learning fast, contributing quickly, and already adding real value. Their fresh perspectives and enthusiasm are helping us support the business even more effectively.

After visiting our Hamilton and Auckland teams, I've been genuinely impressed by the depth of knowledge, the willingness to improve, and the commitment to doing things better. Our value of “We Will Sort It”—challenging the status quo and keeping our eye on tomorrow—is clearly alive in the way our people work every day. I'm excited to be part of this journey.

Take Care,
John.

 
 

Winning of the EnviroNZ RFP - Sales and Aftersales working together


Five years ago the team at TransDiesel were successful winning a request for procurement agreement with EnviroNZ. Since that time, both organisations have achieved some great wins as part of a growing partnership.
Recently, the original contract came to an end and TDX needed to go through a fresh round of discussions to compete for the EnviroNZ business. To find out more about the process, we had a chat with Joe Gradwell, General Manager Sales and Marketing.


From small beginnings, our relationship with EnviroNZ has certainly built over time. It's a nationwide contract so involves all TDX branches across our network, it's fair to say everyone at TDX is involved in some way, shape or form with this key customer stretching from parts to service and repair. They provide a continuous stream of work across our business and feed revenue into all areas of TDX.

At TDX we pride ourselves on customer acquisition, but also customer retention. It's a team effort to ensure we're all working together to deliver the very best experience for our customers.

This is especially true when it comes to EnviroNZ. By the very nature of their business, the processing of waste and hazardous materials, there is a tendency for gear to have a very hard life.

It's vital for them that we're able to respond quickly to breakages and breakdowns and, thanks to everyone at TDX supporting each other, we've become really skilled in fulfilling fast turnaround needs and requirements. In some cases this involves servicing outside of usual business hours, ensuring maximum uptime of the EnviroNZ fleet. So a massive thank you to everyone who goes above and beyond.

The relationship we have with EnviroNZ extends from the coalface to the boardroom, we enjoyed shared respect no matter whether we're doing a repair in the field in the cold and the mud, or meeting with their executives - key to all interactions is communication. The more we can talk with them, and then with each other, only helps strengthen the relationship.

The work by the entire team across the country is certainly noticed. As is the support and backup provided by people in various areas of the business. As has been demonstrated, if we can get the fundamentals at the coalface right, then it helps create a sales funnel and leads to other opportunities. I'm pleased to say this is definitely the case with EnviroNZ.

It's this ability to deliver which has helped develop such a strong partnership with EnviroNZ, so much so that we were their preferred supplier going into the latest supplier procurement round.

I'm pleased to say that TDX was successful in winning a new five-year contract, which is a huge independent endorsement for us and our business, and all of our teams across the country who support this customer and make things happen, this comes back to everyone doing their roles well - whether it's admin, service, parts, management or another part of the team.

Winning this RFP again, for another five year term, provides a great sales pipeline of new equipment over the coming years, which is great for our company.

But now the hard work really starts. We can never be complacent and think we have things sewn up for the future, we need to maintain a point of difference to differentiate ourselves from our competition. Our competition keeps moving forward and will be gunning for us, wanting to jump at any opportunity.

So we need to keep innovating, stepping up and delivering a point of difference that makes all of our customers realise and appreciate that TDX really knows what they're doing, that we go above and beyond and exceed expectations.

There's a lot of work which goes on in the background to ensure these partnerships work, so I'd like to acknowledge the Sales and Aftersales team in particular for all the outstanding assistance they provide.

It's the great people and strong team we have at TDX which is one of our strengths and I want to finish by expressing my sincere gratitude to everyone involved with this contract and thank you for delivering. Here's to the next five years - and beyond!
 

Rural NZ Fire Service Re-gear Project


When the Fire Service approached TDX midway through last year with a persistent drivetrain problem, it landed on the desk of Scotty Tomkies, Allison Transmission Business Manager for TDX in Auckland. Rural Fire NZ had been experiencing premature failures with the OEM Manual gearbox in hilly operations across different parts of the country, creating operational risk and downtime the brigade simply couldn't afford.

Rather than applying a short-term fix, Scotty and the TDX team took a structured engineering approach. The brief was clear: deliver a robust, fit-for-purpose solution that would improve reliability and performance in demanding rural conditions. The answer was to re-gear the truck with a fully integrated Allison automatic transmission package.

What followed was a ten-week design and engineering programme. Mounting systems, driveline alignment, cooling requirements, electronic integration and compliance documentation were all carefully developed and validated. Just as importantly, the team documented every stage of the process to future-proof the conversion pathway.

The result exceeded expectations. According to the Fire Service, the upgraded truck delivered improved drivability, smoother operation, and the reliability they had been seeking. Feedback from operators on the ground was overwhelmingly positive.

The ultimate endorsement came quickly: a second Rural Fire Truck from the Taranaki region was sent to TDX for the same conversion. This time, thanks to the groundwork already completed, the project was turned around in just two weeks. With the engineering blueprint established, the team could focus purely on installation, calibration and testing.

For Scotty, the project represents more than a successful technical outcome. It demonstrates the value of listening closely to the customer, applying disciplined engineering, and delivering a solution that not only resolves the original issue but improves overall performance. It's a strong example of how collaboration and technical expertise translate into real-world impact for frontline services.

 

Feature: Mark Geddes, LDX Technician


Mark Geddes has been part of LDX since day one. As one of the original members of the LDX start-up team, he has played a hands-on role in shaping the standards, responsiveness and customer focus that define the business today.


Based in Hamilton, Mark is the dedicated LDX Technician at Fonterra's Crawford Street site, supporting one of the most demanding operational environments in the country. The site runs 24 hours a day, seven days a week, and Mark oversees the maintenance and servicing of approximately 50-60 machines. It's a workload that requires technical competence, discipline and strong time management, all qualities he demonstrates consistently.

Fonterra holds Mark in high regard, and that respect has been earned through reliability and results. He is known for getting in, assessing the situation quickly, and getting the job done properly. He works hard, self-manages effectively and ensures equipment availability remains high in a production environment where downtime is not an option.

While Hamilton is his home base, Mark has recently been supporting the team in Whangārei, stepping in to assist when additional capability was needed. He is often the first to offer help, reinforcing the collaborative culture that underpins LDX and TDX operations.

Customer satisfaction is a priority for Mark. He goes out of his way to ensure expectations are not only met but exceeded, consistently reflecting LDX and TDX values in the way he works and represents the business onsite.

An interesting footnote: Mark owns a DeLorean, reminiscent of the iconic car from Back to the Future. Although the vehicle might suggest Doc Brown, Mark bears a closer resemblance to Michael J. Fox — practical, approachable and always ready for the next challenge.

 

Hero Staff Member - Andrew Falloon


Andrew Falloon has been part of TransDiesel/ TDX for almost 24-years, but in the past 18-months or so his efforts have been particularly outstanding in terms of team management and stepping up when times get tough. He's our 'Hero a Staff Member' for this month, so we took the opportunity to chat with his manager, Joe Gradwell, to find out some more about what has made Andrew's efforts so appreciated.

Andrew's official title is 'Volvo Product and South Island Accounts Manager/ Regional Sales,' but in reality he's so much more than that. His knowledge across the business built up over more than two decades is simply outstanding, he is widely respected by key accounts and brand partners alike.

He's a genuine good bloke and is great with customers. He often wears four or five different hats and can turn his hand at anything. He's the guy who steps up when times get challenging and does whatever it takes to get the job done.

There's no sugar coating the fact we're coming off the back of a difficult period which has seen a number of personnel changes in the TDX business. To his credit, Andrew took on extra work to ensure the Sales team kept pushing forward to keep delivering, often coming at the cost of his own personal time. At all times, he showed care and empathy for his team members and has done his very best to keep the wheels turning across the Volvo team.

Andrew has a world of knowledge, a great understanding of our products and is often the go-to person for a number of people. With Andrew, the customer is always front of mind and he will ensure he has every i dotted and t crossed. He always fronts up and delivers to both TDX team members and our customers.

Andrew's workload has been very large at times due to trying to wear multiple hats, often when dealing with brand partners overseas with the time zone challenges there is a lot of additional work that happens at night also when everyone is fast asleep here in New Zealand. For example, the likes of Volvo wake up to start their day in Sweden, in some cases with meetings running into the early hours of the morning.

I want to take a moment to thank Andrew for the work that he has done, and also our wider sales team for bearing with us while we have navigated an extremely challenging period, we are not fully out of this yet however we are seeing a number of positive signs.

We are currently adding some resource around Andrew to assist with the workload. Chris has hit the ground running and I know this will help us as a sales team take another step forward, especially with Andrew as his mentor as there is no one better to learn from and it is a fantastic opportunity to gain skills from an exceptionally well-experienced professional.

Andrew is a quiet achiever, he gets on with the job without any fuss or drama and is well respected inside and out of the business. As his manager, I'm certainly grateful and privileged to have him as part of my team.

Andrew - thank you - as you certainly deserve to be recognised and have earned a massive pat on the back.

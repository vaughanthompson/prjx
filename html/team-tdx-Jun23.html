Collaboration key to success at TDX

Joe Gradwell: General Manager - Aftersales Operations


Thanks, team, what an outstanding finish to the end of the financial year, with sales 1% ahead of our company budget and 11% up on last year. We also exceeded our EBIT budget by 1% which is fantastic - job well done!

I want to recognise people in the network and branch support, everyone contributed to a result that was bigger and better than anticipated. This is only achievable due to the great people we have here at TDX.

Special mention needs to go to the Sales team, who have continued to grow our market share in a declining market. As a supplier, we have retained number #1 in the market - Staying ahead of the pack.

Backing up their efforts is our workshops

At one stage they were pumping through one machine every 90 minutes for the month of March. If you look at the 899 machines sold across the entire year, this means we had a machine being prepared for delivery every 140 minutes across the country, which is a phenomenal achievement in anyone's books.

It's this collaboration across TDX between Support, Sales and Aftersales which builds confidence for our customers, take any one of these away and it all falls over. It also helps keep our relationship with suppliers strong.

This collaboration is also on display amongst the executive, and senior leadership team. It's been a great first year with Colm at the helm and, under his guidance, there are lots of great ideas and innovative solutions being discussed and implemented.

One of these is the new Business Improvements role. This position relates to our brand promise of delivering a world-class experience, so we're putting our money where our mouth is and walking the talk.

Part of this role is a new set of operating standards and how we implement them out at the coal face, designed to introduce consistency across all branches. Karl Scragg will go into more detail later in this update. However, I just want to pay a special mention to Karl for taking on the new challenge, he brings a wealth of knowledge and experience with him and helps TDX be better.

We have also now had over 200 of our team complete the “Customer Centric Training” facilitated by Rob Peterson, there has been some great feedback from these seasons, and I am excited to see how the learnings from this training are implemented across the business to help us deliver a 'world-class experience' for our customers.

And finally, I'd like to welcome Victoria Sergel, our new CFO, to TDX. We're excited to have someone of her calibre as part of our team.

In the meantime, thanks again for all your efforts - they're noticed and much appreciated.

Thank you.

 

New Business Improvements role looking for new efficiencies across TDX

As with any business, there are often areas that can be fine-tuned to deliver even better results. Established at the start of May, the new Business Improvements role is intended to find those places which can deliver some real gains.


“It's about identifying opportunities, implementing positive change and seeing if there are other areas of the business which can also benefit,” said Karl Scragg, who has moved from his previous position to be the new Business Improvements Manager.

“These can be across financial, manpower and time considerations. At the end of the day, it's about working smarter and making things better for our team as well as our customers.”

One of the key areas of focus for Karl is developing systems, procedures, and guidelines for TDX across New Zealand.

“These operating standards will deliver consistency throughout all 17 branches. This is important as we introduce new equipment into the market and start gearing up for the future. They will enable us to coach and develop our people, allowing our team to do the absolute best job possible for our customers and also help our key business partners know what they can expect from TDX.

“I'm excited about the opportunity to contribute to the world-class experience on offer from TDX.”

 

TDX Locals Helping Locals community programme partnership with BrainTree 'a phenomenal success beyond all expectations.'


At the heart of the TDX brand promise is 'locals helping locals' and, as part of last year's rebrand project and to help demonstrate this commitment, branches around the country are encouraged to partner with and raise money for local community-based organisations.

Daryl and the southern teams in Invercargill, Dunedin and Cromwell were first out of the blocks helping two great charities in the South, providing support to the Loss and Grief Centre and Life Matters Suicide Prevention Trust.

The Head Office team hatched a plan to raise money for BrainTree, a wellness centre addressing the neurological needs of the community. BrainTree made sense for several reasons: they are local in Christchurch and are privately funded, so rely on local businesses like TDX to support them.

“When we set out on this journey, we were unsure how much could be raised, we were hoping for $30,000-40,000,” said Marc Warr, TDX Marketing Manager.

“The momentum just kept building, we were blown away by online auction items the suppliers put up and the support from customers, particularly on the customer evening at the Hotel Montreal.

“In the end, it was a united approach from our suppliers, customers who support us, buying our products and services and the TDX team who bid on items, shared our videos and posts and bought raffle tickets.

“The generosity and level of support displayed by our customers was amazing, they really enjoyed the opportunity to give back to the community as well.

“The $54,000 raised was more than we ever anticipated and, of all the projects the marketing team has worked on, this one is easily the most rewarding. It's so humbling to be involved in an event such as this, one which makes such a real difference to the lives of so many.”


The donation is a significant amount for BrainTree, it means they can literally keep doing what they do and can continue to help change lives.

“It's an amazing example of locals helping locals and everyone at TDX should feel proud of what we were able to do,” added Marc.

“Hopefully it will inspire other branches to look at what they can do to help their own locals.”

Hero Staff Member - Rob Beale




Energy, attitude, and positive mindset helped Rob win People's Choice at the annual Sales Awards

After many years of being a customer, it took a bit of convincing for Rob Beale to make the jump to TDX a year ago - especially considering he was moving into an unfamiliar sales role. But, since making the big decision to join the team, he's been kicking goals and hasn't looked back.

“Rob has endless energy and enthusiasm, nothing phases him and when faced with a challenge he's already thought about possible solutions,” said Charles Malcolm, General Manager Equipment Sales. He not only lives and breathes TDX values, but what has been phenomenal about Rob's performance is that he's achieved things in one year which can take some people much longer. Even though he's never had sales experience, he's an absolute natural and achieved his budget for the year.

“Holy heck, he can move some steel and if it's not bolted down, he'll sell it. It's this type of all-around attitude and outlook which saw Rob nominated for the People's Choice award and also recognised as the very clear winner.”

Based in Wairoa, Rob is the Equipment Territory Manager for Gisborne/ Hawkes Bay, and like many others in the area was impacted by recent weather events.

“Following the Cyclone Gabrielle floods, Rob was a huge help to his community, getting out on his own Yanmar digger after work to assist with clearing silt and debris from roads in his area,” said Charles.

“This is the type of person he is, an inspiring salt-of-the-earth character who doesn't think twice about helping other people. He deserves this recognition, congratulations to Rob on his success!”

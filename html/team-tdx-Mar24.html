The TDX Taniwhas: Taking on the best in the world!

In Māori mythology, taniwha are large supernatural beings that live in deep pools in rivers, dark caves, or in the sea, especially in places with dangerous currents or giant waves. They may be considered highly respected kaitiaki (protective guardians) of people and places.

This month's newsletter will focus on the TDX Volvo Masters team, known as the TDX Taniwhas, which consists of:



Brent Mitchell - Team Coach (National CE Product Support)

Manny Allgeier - Team Captain (W/S Foreman from Taupo Branch)

Jono Clerke - Team Technician (W/S Foreman from Napier Branch)

Sam Burgess - Team Technician (W/S Field Mechanic from Invercargill Branch)

Jessie Baucke - Team Parts Correspondent (Parts Manager from Taupo Branch)

The TDX Taniwhas won the Region International Finals in Dubai in January and are now preparing for the World Finals, to be held in Sweden on May 6-8.

 
We spoke with Brent Mitchell, Product Support Manager/ Volvo Masters Coach and TDX Taniwha Mentor, about his experience at TDX as well as involvement with the team.


It's fair to say Brent Mitchell knows a thing or two about Volvo products, he's been working on the machines for almost 30 years.


During this time he's seen a huge amount of product development, meaning he's the perfect person to lead the Product Support team and also be involved with the TDX Taniwha as Volvo Masters Coach and Mentor.

“My day-to-day role is Manager of the Product Support team, there are five of us in the team - three in the North Island and two in the South. In a nutshell, we're the after sales support for the team on the front line,” said Brent.

“Between myself, Grant, Mike, Craig and Jason, we have collectively more than 80-years' experience which puts us in a good position to be able to provide recommendations and solutions to issues in the field. We also assist with machine delivery and training.

“Between us we're a tight knit team who play a role in supporting all the products we sell, right across all areas of the business.

“The majority of our role is spent helping with technical issues, but also providing parts and sales support, when and where needed.”

Brent joined TDX in 2011 and has witnessed firsthand the rate of change in equipment and how quickly new technology has been introduced to the market.

“The greatest changes have been in product and engine technology, followed by a big shift towards battery electric technology and machine innovation.

“A lot of the gear today is almost semi-autonomous, which can create challenges for technicians when it comes time to understand and then to calibrate systems.

“Many of our techs do work out in the field, which requires a high level of product know-how plus innovation and adaptability.

“It's this resourcefulness which stands us in good stead when it comes to Volvo Masters competition time, as we're always thinking on our feet to understand a problem and develop a solution.”

Having a handle on technical issues is an advantage which helps Brent when he's coaching the TDX Taniwhas, as it's some of these challenges which TDX teams excel at during Volvo Masters competitions.

“There is a heap of intensive training to prepare for the competition, as it's not just about parts and service - there's aftersales and connected service components too, with a whole lot of analysis involved.”


 

The TDX Taniwhas were selected to represent Oceania at the International Competition in Dubai, where they claimed top spot and an invitation to the World Finals.


Taking place in Sweden in May, the finals will include teams from China, North America, Latin America, Asia, India and Europe (The TDX Taniwhas and one team from Australia form the International group).

12 teams will be competing against each other over two days and will be presented with a variety of tasks which could be performed on any machines from the Volvo product range.

Challenges could be as different as dealing with machines and software, connected service/ telematics through to analysing machine health. Deliberate faults are put into machines, then the team has 45-minutes to troubleshoot and solve the problem.

“Coming from New Zealand, we are one of the smallest countries and dealerships represented and also have the greatest distance to travel to get to the competition,” said Brent.

“It's a big achievement for us to even get to the Finals, we're going to do our best and make TDX proud.”

 
As part of the TDX Taniwhas feature, we also had a chat with Bradley Cooper, the Volvo Service Market Manager for Oceania, to get a bit more information and insight about the prestigious competition - and whether he thinks TDX can claim back-to-back titles.


Internationally renowned as one of the largest competitive assessments of technicians of its kind in the world, the Volvo Construction Equipment Masters is the pinnacle of competition for aftermarket specialists from around the world.

The Masters was launched in 1990 and is a unique event which showcases the skills of parts and service technicians inside Volvo Construction Equipment's global dealer network.

“There are few events of this kind which match the Volvo Masters for engagement on a global scale, the numbers for the competition are absolutely remarkable,” said Bradley.

“There are seven regions represented, consisting of 134 dealers from around the world who have put forward 634 teams with a total of 2,235 participants. With these sorts of numbers competing, to make it through to the finals is a phenomenal achievement.”

As part of the competition, teams need to demonstrate their skills, knowledge and expertise when it comes to all elements of the aftersales operation.

“It's a long road to get to the World Finals,” said Bradley.

The TDX team are in fact the defending Champions, having claimed the title in 2019 - which was the last time the event was held before COVID travel restrictions placed a hold on the competition.

So what does Bradley think about the chances of TDX Taniwhas standing on the podium again in May?

“TDX is a nimble and agile business which demonstrates an integrated approach across their entire operation. They have proven to be capable of adapting to new technology, systems and processes and are highly regarded in the region.

“From a competition perspective, TDX aftersales members have some of the highest completion rates and are amongst the best performing technicians when it comes to online training modules, this shows through in their results at the Masters.

“The team is world-class in terms of training, preparation and the ability to think clearly and concisely in pressure situations, they are also 'outside of the box' thinkers who try different ways to tackle a particular problem. These skills shine through time and time again.

“Good luck and best wishes for the Finals, I have high expectations for the team and look forward to seeing what they're capable of achieving in Sweden in May.”




Here's an introduction from each of the TDX Taniwhas, we hope you enjoy getting to know them a little bit more.


As you'll learn through their videos, getting to the Volvo Masters World Final is a huge accomplishment. If you see the team out and about, please wish them well for the finals, they'll definitely appreciate your support.

As Sam says in his clip, it's very definitely a 'once in a lifetime opportunity' and, backed up by Jono's comment, the team are all 'looking forward to taking on the best of the best in the world.'

Fingers crossed the team bring home the World Finals title!

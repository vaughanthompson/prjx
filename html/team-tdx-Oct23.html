It's challenging out there, but we're up for it...


Hi everyone,

We are now six months into the new Financial Year, and the total equipment market has contracted 20% against last year. It's challenging, yes, but TDX is keeping pace with the market and holding up from a market share perspective, retaining #1 position in the market.

Over the past few months, I've been really impressed with the way everyone across the business has stepped up to the challenge in this challenging market. I have no doubt that we have the right team to keep the momentum going. In times like these, it's about being responsible for managing costs in our individual areas while taking advantage of every opportunity and backing ourselves.

In recent staff appointments, and on behalf of TDX I'd like to welcome Phillip Bracefield to the team. Phil is taking up the position of GM of Supply Chain and brings extensive knowledge and experience to this pivotal role in the business. There's a feature about Phil in this edition.

There has also been a great deal of Volvo-related activity happening over the past month.

We have seen several Volvo hybrid machines arrive in NZ, this forms part of our overall TDX strategy of embracing new technology and it's great to be partnering with a brand like Volvo who is at the forefront of innovation and technology.

We have also come off the back of a successful Volvo visit made by four members of the executive management team from the international region of Volvo. This is the first time they have been to New Zealand to see what we do and meet some of our customers.

Their itinerary included visits to Christchurch, Taupo, Tauranga and Auckland for meetings and site visits with key accounts.

It was an invaluable opportunity for the Volvo team to see what we are doing in NZ, and how developed our market is.

We are pushing to have more pilots in NZ such as the EC480EL hybrid machine at Stevensons Dury, which was the first in the world.

Our customers are ready for the new technology and it is good for the executive management team to see this as it will open up more opportunities for us in the future.

The value of such visits cannot be underestimated and it helps position TDX as a leader in our field and reaffirms to partners such as Volvo that we are serious about what we do and how we do things.

A further example of this growing partnership is Laura Velasquez Sanchez, who was flown to Sweden by Volvo to present our TDX experience with their ActiveCare programme. It was outstanding acknowledgment of Laura and the TDX team that Volvo decided to pay for her to fly to Europe and is recognition of what we're able to achieve in a small and dynamic team, punching above our weight competing on the world stage. Read more about the 'walking conference' she participated in later in this issue.

Brent Mitchell was also hosted by Volvo on a trip to the Uptime Boost event plus side programmes to Scotland to do the Volvo R100 Technical and Operator training, as well as to Germany for Electromobility training.

I wish everyone a great month, thanks for your continued commitment to TDX and all you do in delivering a world-class experience for our customers… and of course good luck to Ireland (and the All Blacks) in the rugby!

Thanks,
Colm.

 

TDX journey with ActiveCare creates opportunity to talk about next level up-time at Volvo event in Sweden

TDX Product Support Analyst Laura Velasquez Sanchez who has been with TDX for nearly a year, has led the implementation and commercialisation of Volvo ActiveCare. Laura was fortunate to have an opportunity to visit Volvo in Sweden recently to participate in a special event where she spoke about our experience with ActiveCare.


“We officially launched ActiveCare in July 2023, but before then had been trialling the machine monitoring system with a small number of our key accounts over the previous two years,” said Laura.

“ActiveCare is a remote and real-time monitoring and telematics solution offered by Volvo, with two core services: 24/7 machine monitoring and weekly reports. The machines send all the information to the uptime Centre in Sweden, generating a case alert only if an action is required.

Volvo ActiveCare offers numerous benefits to our customers, including improved efficiency, reduced operating costs, enhanced safety, tailored recommendations, operating behaviour alerts and better environmental performance. It provides valuable data and insights that can help optimise fleet operations and ensure the units are operating at peak performance.

“Weekly reports provide customer with a detailed overview of their machine performance and operator behaviour, empowering our customers with a potent tool for enhancing their operations and driving positive outcomes.

“Moreover, ActiveCare grants us at TDX an additional dimension of insight into machine operations, enabling us to proactively engage with customers and offer tailored recommendations. It is essentially telematic data working smarter and harder for you.”

With the weekly reports, we can establish a regular touchpoint with our customers, gain insights about their operations and look out for additional opportunities. All the reports are available for TDX staff to read, please check out an upcoming Viva post from Laura relating to their location.

New Zealand is often considered at the forefront of embracing new technologies and it's proving to be no different with ActiveCare.

“TDX is leading our region when it comes to uptake and integration of this system and we are ranked number five in the world in terms of connected machines,” said Laura.

“There are currently 371 different Volvo units in operation around the country which are actively monitored as part of the ActiveCare telematics service.

“It's this sort of market cut through which resulted in Volvo asking us to be part of their recent Uptime Support Boost event, which was held in Sweden in early September.”

“I did my presentation eight times over three days to about 250 people, describing the TDX journey with Volvo ActiveCare.

“I'd like to acknowledge everyone involved in the programme, it's a real team effort bringing this technology to life, as feedback from technicians, service advisors, and our Product support team from across the country has been critical to the success of the system.

“Above all else, our involvement with ActiveCare is another example of the level of service we're offering our customers and demonstrates we have their back when it comes to offering new and innovative technologies which improve how they operate.”

 

Classic cars and a Yanmar digger part of the journey to TDX


Phil Bracefield is the new General Manager, Supply Chain at TDX and starts his new role on Monday 30 October. We took the opportunity to have a quick chat with him before he gets his feet under the desk and pulls on a piece of high-vis.

Hi Phil, we're looking forward to welcoming you at TDX, can you tell us a bit about your working background?

Well, there's quite a bit of history, including a link to the original TDX when they were known as Transmission and Diesel. I was a young chap in my 20's working as an ENZED Hose Doctor, this was my first connection to the heavy construction industry and on occasion I dealt with the team when repairing diggers. I am mechanically minded so enjoyed the challenge of getting machines back up and running, I still enjoy operating machinery to this day.

More recently, I've been involved in the Agri and manufacturing sectors for 23 years across a variety of executive and GM positions as well as director of supply chain operations, so bring an extensive range of experience to TDX.

What attracted you to a job at TDX?

It's a combination of multiple factors, primarily an amazing opportunity to bring together a number of employment passions into a role in the heavy construction industry.

TDX has an enviable reputation and numerous supplier relationships at a national and international level with some of the biggest names in the business, which is something that really appeals to me.

The new role provides the ability to put skills to work into a job I'm going to be incredibly passionate about.

What special skills do you bring to the role that can help manage these challenging moments?

I am very data driven, I have a real interest in numbers and insights they can provide to help paint a picture of a situation or scenario, and then balanced by reviewing with the various teams via the sensibility check. It's difficult to make an informed decision without all the information, so I'm someone who will ensure every piece of data is considered before making the final call.

What do you enjoy doing when you're not at work?

I have been heavily involved with motorsport over the years, including circuit racing at Ruapuna and Teretonga, plus club rallying.

However, I've hung up the racing boots and now love to indulge my passion for classic cars. I have a couple in the garage including a 1960 VW Beetle - which has been judged best in the country three times at the VW Nationals - a BA Falcon GT-P and my current project is a Factory 1966 VW Kombi Split Screen Double Cab Ute.

I'm very family focussed, I have four daughters and I love spending time with them and their families, including three granddaughters.

My partner and I live on a lifestyle block just outside of Christchurch, there is a fair bit of work which needs doing on it so by a happy coincidence I purchased a Yanmar digger almost one year ago. It's been a great introduction to the product and I enjoy settling into the driver's seat and ticking off jobs around the property.

It's been great chatting with you, Phil - do you have any final comments?

I'm really excited about the upcoming TDX opportunity. I'm exhilarated to be joining such a dynamic team and am confident I can bring some great skills to the table which will help TDX on our future journey.

I look forward to getting things underway on October 30 and meeting the rest of the team!

Back in the TDX team after a brief break away


The team at TDX Tauranga recently welcomed one of their former crew back to the business after a brief break away, when Parts Correspondent Julia Treloar restarted at the Mt Maunganui site on July 7.


“I left TDX for about one and a half years when I went to a role in the auto industry, but I just didn't feel 100% about the change so had been looking for an opportunity to rejoin,” said Julia.

“There are two main drivers for restarting with TDX, the first is the potential of career progression and personal development, while the second is the chance to work with some great people.

“There are three of us in the Tauranga TDX branch looking after parts, we all pitch in to get the job done, ranging from picking parts, answering calls and replying to emails.

“It's a really positive team-focussed environment, one in which I feel really comfortable and enjoy coming to work.”

The chance for customer interaction and learning what different industries need in terms of parts support, and how TDX can help provide local know-how, is also something which has appeal for Julia.

“It's great being able to get to know customers and share knowledge with them, also to help find solutions to any issues they might be experiencing,” she said.




And does Julia have any advice for people considering a move to TDX, or rejoining after some time away?

“Just go for it, apply for the role and become part of the TDX team.

“It's fantastic to be back.”

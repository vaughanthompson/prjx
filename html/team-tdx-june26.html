Breaking News: TDX recognised at Global Yanmar Dealer Conference for Sales, Aftersales and Overall Dealer Performance.


TDX has been recognised on the international stage, taking top honours for Sales Excellence, placing second in Aftersales Excellence, and achieving third overall for Dealer Performance at a recent Yanmar Dealer Conference, where dealers from across the Yanmar network gathered to share best practices and celebrate success.

TDX's National Sales Manager - Yanmar, Richard Papps, and General Manager of Sales and Marketing, Joe Gradwell, were invited to present two of the company's standout success stories to fellow Yanmar dealers, showcasing initiatives that have helped strengthen Yanmar's position in New Zealand and enhance customer support nationwide.

Richard presented TDX's sales success story, centred around the strategic rollout of dedicated Yanmar Sales Specialists across key regions including Canterbury, Auckland and Waikato. The initiative has fundamentally reshaped TDX's capability and competitive strength, delivering market share growth of up to 41 percent in some regions and playing a significant role in Yanmar becoming New Zealand's number one compact machinery brand by 2022.

The specialist model has enabled greater product expertise, stronger customer relationships, improved regional focus and a more targeted sales strategy, resulting in sustained growth and market leadership across multiple key regions.

Joe's presentation focused on TDX's innovative Yanmar Fixed Price Servicing Programme, launched nationally in June 2025. The programme combines Yanmar's SmartAssist technology with automated SMS servicing reminders, proactively notifying customers when machines are approaching service intervals based on operating hours. The initiative has improved customer engagement, increased service bookings and generated strong ongoing aftersales revenue while helping maximise machine uptime.

The presentations resonated strongly with the dealer network, with TDX awarded first place in the Sales Success Story category and second place in the Aftersales Success Story category, based on votes from fellow Yanmar dealers. These achievements also contributed to TDX being recognised as the third-ranked Yanmar dealer overall for Dealer Performance, reflecting the company's consistent excellence across sales, service and customer support.


Richard Papps said the recognition reflects the commitment and expertise of the entire TDX team.

"The success of the Yanmar specialist model demonstrates what can be achieved when you invest in capability, customer relationships and deep product expertise. Our dedicated Yanmar specialists have become trusted advisors to customers and strong advocates for the brand. It's incredibly rewarding to see this approach recognised by our Yanmar peers from around the world."

Joe Gradwell said the aftersales initiative was developed with a clear focus on customer outcomes.

"The Fixed Price Servicing Programme is all about making it easier for customers to keep their machines operating at peak performance. By combining technology, automation and proactive communication, we've created a scalable solution that delivers real value for customers while strengthening our service offering. This recognition reinforces our commitment to delivering a world-class customer experience."

TDX's achievements align closely with the company's core value of being the Partner of Choice for the brands that the company represents. The business continues to invest in innovative sales and service initiatives that support customers throughout the lifecycle of their equipment while delivering exceptional outcomes for global brand partners such as Yanmar.

Inoue-san, Manager, Yanmar CE AOLA Division, praised TDX's contribution to the dealer network.

"TDX presented two great initiatives that demonstrate what is possible through strong customer focus, innovation and execution. They are a world-class Yanmar Construction Equipment dealer, and the stories shared by Richard and Joe provided valuable ideas and insights for fellow Yanmar dealers across our network."

The recognition further strengthens TDX's reputation as a leading distributor and service provider of construction equipment in New Zealand and highlights the company's ongoing commitment to delivering world-class service for Yanmar and all of its brand partners. Achieving first place for Sales Excellence, second place for Aftersales Excellence and third overall for Dealer Performance demonstrates the strength of TDX's customer-focused approach and its standing within the global Yanmar dealer network.

TDX Delivers Specialised Solution for QRS Quarry Operation


Quality Roading and Services (QRS) is a specialist civil contracting business based in Wairoa, Hawke's Bay, delivering a wide range of services including infrastructure development, temporary traffic management, quarrying, concrete batching, Hex-Loc manufacturing and installation, and roading. The business is wholly owned by the Wairoa District Council.


TDX recently supplied a specialised excavator to one of QRS's quarry operations, where it is being used to extract high-quality aggregate for the production of concrete products.

The quarry presented a unique challenge. The aggregate is located below the water table in an area close to the coastline, meaning the site is affected by brackish water conditions. Groundwater from the surrounding hill country mixes with seawater pushed inland by tidal movement, creating a higher-salinity environment that adds complexity to extraction activities because the water table sits only a couple of meters below the surface.

Previously, QRS used a dragline for extraction, but this was unable to access the deeper areas where the highest-value aggregate was located. The excavation was shallow on either end of the dragline, and deep in the middle. A long-reach excavator was introduced, allowing deeper and more consistent excavation across the site. However, the team knew they had only one opportunity to access the material — because the excavation area is underwater, there is no easy way to get at it a second time.

With the most valuable material located at the maximum reach of the existing long-reach excavator, QRS began working with TDX to develop a solution that would improve dig depth to maximise the quarry's potential.

The conversation started in 2023, with several options explored before identifying the long-reach excavator as the preferred solution. An initial proposal for an EC380EL with Long Reach (11.4m boom, 8.7m arm) was developed, but the project was temporarily placed on hold.

When discussions resumed, safety considerations became a key focus — particularly ensuring the machine could operate safely without the risk of undermining itself or falling into the excavation area. The final solution incorporated a low cost heavy-duty 60-tonne-rated tether cable anchored to another machine, providing an additional level of security during operation.

Following Board approval, the factory order was placed for an EC380DL with a 11.4m boom, 8.7m arm. The project involved a range of custom-made components, requiring several months for manufacturing, shipping, assembly at TDX Cambridge, and final transport to the quarry site.

Seeing the machine operating for the first time was a significant milestone for everyone involved. The excavator immediately accessed the deeper, high-value material, delivering results from day one and helping unlock the full potential of the quarry operation. A full 2-meters of additional dig depth was achieved from the previous machine, with a larger bucket.

The productivity improvement was significant.

TDX Sales, Volvo Product Management, and TDX Product support all played a vital role in securing this deal. This project is a great example of TDX working collaboratively with a customer to understand a unique challenge and deliver a tailored solution. The result is a highly specialised machine performing exactly as intended — creating real value for QRS and demonstrating the capability of TDX's team to deliver innovative outcomes.

Supply Chain Update: Navigating Global Pressures and Strengthening Our Position


There's no denying it — the global supply chain remains under pressure. Between geopolitical tension, long lead times, and shifting demand, we're operating in a challenging environment. Here's a quick update on what we're seeing, what it means for us in New Zealand, and where the team is focusing.


Global Pressures (and why they matter to us)

The ongoing situation in the Middle East continues to ripple through global supply chains. It's affecting key shipping routes, fuel markets, and overall freight stability — all of which ultimately flow through to us.

For Shell and TDX, these pressures flow through to increased product cost, and reduced availability. This reinforces the need for disciplined planning and strong supplier engagement to manage the ever-evolving landscape.

New Zealand: The Last Bus Stop

New Zealand's geographic position places us at the end of the global supply chain — effectively the “last bus stop.”

    This means:
  • We are often the final allocation point after global demand is met
  • Supply disruptions upstream are amplified by the time they reach us
  • Recovery time from shortages can be significantly longer than in other markets

This reality requires us to take a proactive and highly coordinated approach to planning, ordering, and stock management.


Inventory Position: Balancing Availability and Risk

A big focus for Supply Chain is making sure we've got the right stock in the right place — without overloading ourselves with excess inventory.

    It's a constant balancing act:
  • We need enough stock to keep operations running smoothly
  • But too much stock ties up cash and creates other risks

There's no perfect formula here, but the goal is to stay as aligned as possible with actual demand.


Long lead times = no room for guesswork

Lead times of up to five months are now common across many supplier channels, particularly from our European supply base. That makes accurate, data driven, forecasting and forward planning more important than ever. Because of that, forecasting isn't something supply chain can (or should) do alone. We really need input from across the business to get this right.

    That means:
  • Sharing insights early
  • Flagging changes in demand quickly
  • Avoiding decisions being made in isolation

The more aligned we are upfront, the fewer surprises we have later.


Building Stronger Supplier Partnerships

A key focus area is the development of national supply agreements to consolidate our spend and strengthen our purchasing position within the New Zealand market.

Rather than relying on fragmented, ad hoc purchasing, we're working to consolidate spend and build more strategic supplier relationships.

    The upside:
  • Better buying power
  • More reliable supply
  • Stronger relationships over the long term
  • Less reactive procurement

It's about playing the long game and setting ourselves up for more stability.

Recent Team Changes

Following the recent Sales team update and July McQueen's move into the Sales Operations Support Manager role, the Supply Chain Administration team of Angela Roberts and Rhiannon Robson have now moved into Procurement. This brings the teams closer together and creates a broader Supply Chain function that manages the process from planning and purchasing through to invoicing.

People are at the heart of how the Supply Chain team operates, and I want to sincerely thank the team for their hard work, commitment, and the way they continue to support the business every day in a challenging environment.


What the team is focused on
    Right now, the supply chain team is focused on a few key areas:
  • Improving forecasting through better business engagement
  • Supporting recent team updates and strengthening alignment across the broader Supply Chain function
  • Building inventory resilience to improve availability while managing stock risk
  • Keeping a close eye on global risks and adjusting where needed

Closing

It's a challenging environment — no question about it — but we're taking practical steps to stay ahead of it.

The biggest lever we've got is collaboration. The earlier we're talking, sharing, and aligning, the better the outcome for everyone.

If you've got changes coming up, new projects, or shifts in demand — loop us in early. It makes a big difference.

TDX makes a big impact at record-breaking Fieldays


Fieldays 2026 proved to be a standout event for TDX, with the team making the most of a spectacular presence at Mystery Creek in Hamilton from June 10-13.


The Southern Hemisphere's largest agricultural event attracted a record crowd of more than 132,000 visitors over four days, providing an ideal platform for TDX to showcase its industry-leading brands, connect with customers, and build new relationships.

TDX's large three-berth stand was a hive of activity throughout the event and featured Volvo, FUCHS and Yanmar. Richly deserved compliments should go to the team involved in the design as well as standard of presentation, as it set the foundation for what was another successful appearance at Fieldays for TDX.

Northern Sales Manager Nigel Davies said the event was a fantastic opportunity to showcase the business and the people behind the brands.

“I was very proud of the team. Their engagement with show-goers was amazing, and their performance was head and shoulders above other nearby stands,” Nigel said.

“With a team of up to 11 staff working across the event, everyone was motivated, always out front, and talking with visitors. It was super busy, but the team really stepped up.”

Nigel expects the Fieldays presence will translate into strong business opportunities, with around 30 deals anticipated as a result of conversations held during the show.

“Fieldays is always a great opportunity to raise brand awareness, but it's also valuable for gathering intelligence about our competitors and understanding any challenges their customers may be facing. Those conversations give us a chance to introduce our products and highlight where we can add value.”

Beyond the commercial outcomes, Fieldays also provided a great opportunity for the TDX team to come together and strengthen its culture. Three members of the Canterbury team travelled north to be part of the event, adding to the collaborative spirit across the business.

There were plenty of memorable moments, including the ever-popular kids' sandpit, which once again proved a huge hit with families - and a bit of a challenge when it came time to wrestle the toys off the kids at the end of each day.


The team also handed out 400 bags of pork crackling as a unique giveaway, creating plenty of smiles and leaving visitors with a memorable reminder of TDX.

Overall, Fieldays 2026 was a tremendous success, with the TDX team doing an outstanding job of representing the company and putting its key brands in front of thousands of people.

"A huge thank you to everyone involved in making this event such a success. The Marketing team did an outstanding job with the planning and setup, providing a fantastic platform for the event.

The Sales team then carried that momentum throughout, engaging with customers and representing TDX exceptionally well.

It was a bloody good effort from everyone involved, and I sincerely appreciate the commitment, teamwork, and hard work that made it all happen. Thanks again to the entire team." — Joe Gradwell, GM Sales & Marketing

Hero Staff Member - Dan Parks. Taking the next step in his TDX journey


This month's Hero Staff Member is a fantastic example of the opportunities that exist at TDX and the success that can come from promoting from within.


Dan Parks' journey is a great reminder that there are many varied, interesting and exciting career pathways available across the business. Often the biggest step is simply being prepared to seize an opportunity when it presents itself.

We caught up with Jason Steele to talk about Dan's move into the Aftersales Territory Manager team and how he has made the transition from workshop-based mechanic to customer-focused commercial professional.

In the middle of last year, changes were made to the structure of the Aftersales Business sales team. With overlapping roles and responsibilities creating some duplication in customer contact, the team identified an opportunity to streamline the approach and make better use of everyone's time.

The Customer Service Representative (CSR) role was reshaped and rebranded as Aftersales Territory Managers, creating six new positions across the country: three in the North Island and three in the Mainland.

At the start of 2026, an opportunity became available to fill the Central South Island role, and the quality of internal applicants stood out as being very high. Ultimately, Dan was selected for the position, taking the next step in his TDX career.

Dan started with TDX supporting our EQD as a yardman, before taking on an adult apprenticeship through the Christchurch workshop. Not one to rest on his laurels, once qualified he then continued his training by progressing into his Auto Electrical apprenticeship.

While the move meant swapping his overalls for a tucked-in shirt, Dan has transferred the same passion, energy, and problem-solving approach from the workshop into his new commercial role.

“Dan has a real knack for developing and maintaining relationships,” Jason said.

“Dan will be the first one to admit he was a little out of his comfort zone, but he's taken the role and is running with it - developing opportunities and helping customers in their business. He's applying his mechanical approach to business, and it's working.”


Dan's approach is simple but effective: have good conversations, ask good questions, do what you say you will and always follow up with customers.

That mindset has already delivered results, with Dan identifying opportunities across areas such as Fulton Hogan with loader cutting edges, and even convincing Road Metals to convert from Castrol oil to Volvo genuine oils. He is helping customers understand what TDX can offer and creating repeat business opportunities.

“He's exceeding my expectations,” Jason said.

“Dan is developing his skillset, building credibility and trust in the market and adding real value. He's doing a bloody good job.”

Dan's success is a great example of the career opportunities available across TDX. With the right attitude, willingness to learn and the drive to make a difference, there are pathways for everyone to step up and deliver.


We're all thrilled to see Dan's success and look forward to following his continued growth.

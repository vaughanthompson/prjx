Introduction by Joe Gradwell, GM Sales & Marketing.


Welcome team to another TDX and LDX newsletter, this is the first introduction I have done in my new position as General Manager of Sales and Marketing.


I've only been in the role a few weeks and my initial focus is understanding what's working well and identifying areas which need some attention. My job is to lead the team and get ready for the market as it improves, so we can function the best we can in our industry, for ourselves as well as our customers.

There have been challenging times of late but there is light at the end of the tunnel. I, together with the ELT, are confident we are going to be well placed to hit the ground running and claim some impressive wins as the market comes back.

Personally, I'm really excited to move across to the Sales team after a career in Aftersales. And I'm also hugely proud to be part of TDX and what is undoubtedly a home-grown NZ success story.

We're fortunate to represent a wide selection of globally recognised brands which cover a wide and diverse range of industries. This, along with our people is the strength of TDX. We don't have all our eggs in one basket and this helps offset any slow-downs which might be experienced across various sectors.

This is a really exciting time for TDX, there are a number of new products and recent developments, including:

  • Volvo Next Gen Hauler - Launched, with the first unit delivered to Birchfield Ross Mining.

  • Volvo F Series Excavator - Now in market, with loaders expected by year-end.

  • Yanmar 86-7 Compact Excavator - First unit delivered and already generating strong interest.

  • FUCHS - Officially welcomed to our brand portfolio, with two units ordered and positive customer feedback.

  • Shell Panolin - Recently launched, with increasing excitement around this biodegradable product range.

  • Power Products - Excellent work in maintaining our market-leading position, driven by our exclusive partnership with FrostBoss.

These new products are exciting, however their success will come down to how we work together as a cohesive team. We shouldn't loose focus on continuing to deliver a world-class experience backed with local know-how that our customers have come to expect from us.

It's my goal to build a more of a strategic approach to how we deal with customers, and make it even easier to do business with us from their perspective.

It's also my pleasure to welcome Greg Slaughter as our new CEO. Greg has spent his first few weeks learning about the business, meeting brand partners, and visiting branches and key customers. I'm excited to work alongside him as we continue to strengthen TDX.

Thank you to everyone's dedication over recent months. It will take a true team effort — across parts, service, and sales — to continue to navigate this challenging market, and we can only succeed if we work together.
 

TDX Celebrates Success at Shell APAC Dealer Conference


In August, Jason Steele and Marc Warr had the opportunity to attend the Shell Dealer Meeting Conference in Bangkok. The three-day event brought together around ten distributors from across the APAC region, alongside Shell's regional and global marketing and sales teams. It was a fantastic chance to learn, share ideas, and celebrate success with colleagues from across the network.


Day One set the tone with in-depth discussions about sales and marketing strategies across key sectors such as fleet and transport, construction, power, and industrial. These sessions were not only about sharing Shell's global approach but also gave each distributor the chance to reflect on their own markets.

For TDX, it was a great opportunity to check in on our local strategies, note what's working, and see where we can apply learnings from other regions.

The day ended on a high with an awards dinner, where Jason and Marc proudly accepted the Award of Excellence for Q2 Best Oceania Sales Growth. This recognition really highlights the effort and dedication of the entire TDX team in supporting and growing the Shell brand in New Zealand.

Day Two shifted focus to Shell's grease portfolio, an area that presents some exciting opportunities for TDX in the New Zealand market. The team also took part in case study presentations, which gave everyone a closer look at how different regions are approaching growth.

TDX shared insights into e-commerce marketing, an especially relevant topic as we continue improving our Tradedoor portal and digital strategies. Hearing from other regions about their digital marketing efforts sparked plenty of ideas that we can build on at home.

Day Three wrapped up the event with a tour of a Shell blending plant in Bangkok. Seeing the production processes up close offered a better appreciation of the scale and precision behind Shell's operations. It was a fitting way to finish, grounding the big-picture discussions in the real-world production that supports them.

Overall, the Bangkok conference was a brilliant opportunity to connect with other markets, strengthen our partnership with Shell, and celebrate the results of our hard work. Bringing home the Award of Excellence was a proud moment and a great reminder of what we can achieve together. A big thanks to the whole TDX team—your efforts made this recognition possible!



It's safe to say, TDX has a great relationship and is well respected as a Shell distributor. This is a credit to the entire TDX team and all we continue to do for the Shell brand here in New Zealand.
 

Kohler Marine Unveils Groundbreaking In-House Parallel Generator Control System


Kohler Marine has developed a control panel that is helping to redefine power management in the marine industry.

“Traditionally, boats have relied on third-party controllers and complex interfaces to link multiple generators, but Kohler's in-house solution now delivers seamless parallel and syncing functionality with just three wires,” explained Scott McAlpine, TDX Technical Support - Marine Products Manager.

“This innovation allows operators to link two or more generators, automatically managing load requirements by starting and stopping units as needed. For example, if a vessel is drawing 100kW at 80% load, the controller will automatically bring a second generator online, increasing available capacity to 200kW, with the ability to add more power as demand grows. When power requirements drop, the system seamlessly shuts down unneeded units, maximising efficiency and minimising wear.”

The advantages are significant. By managing smaller generators in parallel, operators can reduce running costs, fuel consumption, and emissions compared with using a single large engine. Maintenance is also simplified, as service can be spread across multiple smaller units. Importantly, the system provides built-in redundancy, ensuring vessels seldom experience a 'black ship' scenario, with power restored within seconds if a generator goes offline.

This is particularly groundbreaking in the marine sector, where managing four, six, or even eight generators in parallel has previously been impractical. From 15-metre pleasure boats running twin 13.5kW units to superyachts over 40 metres long equipped with 125kW systems, Kohler's solution is already finding applications across both leisure and commercial fleets.

Projects underway include installations at Yachting Developments in West Auckland, where new builds are being equipped with twin 80kW and 125kW systems. Notably, vessels like the late Michael Hill's The Beast, utilise technology and benefit from the system.

With in-house technology eliminating third-party modules, Kohler Marine has delivered a smarter, simpler and more efficient way to keep ships powered, making parallel generation a true 'no-brainer' for the industry.
 

EQD Sales Conference, Christchurch, August 2025.


“The Equipment Division conference was an opportunity to get everyone together as a team and have the chance for a bit of a debrief and a chat about where we've been and where we're heading,” said Joe Gradwell.


“The theme of the conference was 'Perfectly Imperfect' and it set the tone for the meeting. It's important to remember that nothing is perfect, we can only control what we have influence over and qualities such as adaptability and flexibility are vitally important in our business.”

A key outcome of the conference was getting everyone aligned and on the same page, sharing a common goal and emphasising that by working together, it will help make for a better future.

“One area which we know needs improving, and is being worked on, is the Salesforce Platform,” said Joe.

“We are committed to making it a better tool to add benefit to everyone's day-to-day roles. We're ready to address challenges that have been flagged by the team.



“We are also aware of resourcing challenges and will look at the structure to determine what we need going forward, especially as the market comes back - we need to ensure we're responsive and equipped to deliver before we're called on.”

As identified by Joe during the conference, there are many similarities between the All Blacks and TDX.

“We are at the top of our game, we're number one and the competition has us in their sights. The AB's have a new coach, we have a new CEO. They have dropped a few players and changed the composition of the team, we have done the same recently.

Gilbert Enoka, All Blacks mental skills coach, summed it up well when he said - “The capacity to learn is a gift; the ability to learn is a skill; the willingness to learn is a choice.” As a team, learning skills, processes, and how to use technology will help us deliver a world-class experience for our customers, and ultimately leading to more sales for TDX.

“Like the AB's, we need to adapt, get game fit and determine how we stay in front. I have every confidence that we can do it.”

In closing, Joe had some final comments.

“I would like to thank the EQD management team for their help and support, as well as Andrew and Richard for stepping up while we had no leadership/management in place.

“The market is starting to show more confidence; we have a number of quotes going out and our forward orders are building. This is a good sign, and we just need the government to switch on some large projects which will then flow on to equipment sales.

“We have continued to hold our number one market share position in an extremely challenging market; this is something that the entire team at TDX should be extremely proud of.

“So, thank you all for your commitment and hard work. I'm proud to be part of this team, and I'm looking forward to what we will achieve together.”

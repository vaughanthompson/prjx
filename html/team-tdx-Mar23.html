TDX on the world stage

This month's introduction is from Charles Malcolm, GM of Equipment Sales, who recently completed a fact-finding and relationship building business trip with Colm and Joe Gradwell.


" I was privileged to participate in a trip to Europe to visit some key TDX partners based in Europe, as well as see some equivalent dealership facilities.

What immediately blew me away was the size and scale of everything we encountered, the volume of machines produced is astounding. For example, the Volvo Construction Equipment facility in Motherwell produces 120 100-Tonne Rigid Haulers every year, that's one every two days, Sennebogen produce seven Material handlers every day and the Perkins factory in Peterborough produces a new diesel engine every minute. That means it takes them 10-hours to produce New Zealand's total annual Perkins requirements!

" While the volumes are undoubtedly impressive, what was more so was the way in which we were welcomed and the partnerships we were able to create.

In a post-Covid world of Teams calls being the new norm, I was reminded of how incredibly important it is to get out into the field and meet people face to face. People genuinely appreciate it when others make the effort to travel to see them, whether it's across town, intercity or the other side of the world.

These partnerships will be transformational for TDX from a technology standpoint and will help us continue to lead the business and innovation curve here in New Zealand, contributing to our overall customer experience.

When it comes to thinking about the future, many of these operations are looking a long way down the path. Rather than planning for the next three to five years, they're thinking long term, more like 15-50 years, and are building for this sort of time frame.

Sustainability and innovation are also common cornerstones of businesses we visited and reinforced that looking at alternative powertrain solutions is a must. Thankfully, we have some great partners doing amazing work in this space which will enable TDX to provide world class solutions to our customers.

One thing in common for all of these operations, and something which really resonated with me thinking about TDX, is their people - they are passionate, enthusiastic and they love what they do, and they go the extra mile for their customers - just like our team at TDX. I was incredibly heartened to see how TDX stacks up against these European operations, we may be small in the global scheme of things, but we punch above our weight and have a lot to be justifiably proud of. "

 

A Safety Moment from our Newest Branch - Silverdale.

Our new Silverdale branch at 4a Manga Road is part of a block of units. Our neighbours are small businesses who, on the face of things, don't appear to have strong safety policies or procedures.

As part of our Kea Audit, we went to see our neighbours and asked about their emergency evacuation procedure and whether they had a designated assembly point. It became apparent that nothing existed. Following this information, Daniel Teura was nominated as the site Fire Warden (yes, Daniel is trained) and we have purchased some fire horns for our neighbours.

We are going to organise a meeting with our neighbours and prepare a site evacuation plan. We have already put up a sign for the outside assembly point.

It's great that we can spread the safety word and culture as, after all, their safety affects our safety and vice versa.

New GetAccept system delivers world-class quoting solution for TDX


What is GetAccept?

GetAccept is a Configure, Price, and Quoting (CPQ) system, which is linked to Salesforce. It will provide efficiencies and consistency across all TDX departments when providing quotes and other documents to customers. GetAccept is an example of TDX constantly challenging how we do business, improving the customer experience and it will become an important part of the evolution of TDX.

Why does TDX require GetAccept?

Early in 2022, the TDX Equipment Division identified a need to improve the quote creation process for TDX and our customers. Previously, there were too many steps, numerous spreadsheets and a whole crew of people involved to put together a single quote.

We wanted a system that would deliver a professional look and feel, help provide accuracy, enhance time management, and reduce duplication of regular processes.

How does it work?

Linked to an opportunity in Salesforce, GetAccept simplifies what was once a much more complicated process into a few easy steps as it's all standardised through the use of dropdown checkboxes as part of the quote creation process. GetAccept pulls customer, product and pricing data from Salesforce, once approved, the quote is sent directly to the customer via an automatically generated TDX template. The customer can then confirm an order using the digital E-sign functionality. The system provides real time analytics which will allow the Sales team to see how a quote is being viewed and make adjustments to help improve conversion rates across the business.


Mick Skelton with GetAccept Auto Quote System in pilot operation.



When is it getting rolled out?

In the lead-up to the wider introduction of GetAccept, a pilot programme has been running in the Shell Oil Consumables Division since February. The possibilities are endless in terms of application, not just across Consumables, but TDX as a whole. Any document that requires a customer signature could benefit from the GetAccept quoting system.

TDX debuts innovative and interactive new Volvo Demo Zone


Nothing can beat time in the seat, so when it comes to 'try before you buy,' the new interactive Volvo Demo Zone experience for prospective machine operators to put a variety of Volvo products through their paces.

“The great thing about the Volvo Demo Zone is that it allows us to replicate some real-world situations, all here in the one site,” said James O'Connor, Volvo Product Manager at TDX.

“It's another illustration of TDX combining world-class experience with local know-how to provide real benefit to our customers, as firsthand knowledge of a machine far exceeds anything we can ever offer through a brochure.”

“Operating in the one environment means we can remove the variables from a workplace situation, which allows different machines to be compared in the same conditions,” said James.

“For example, we recently demonstrated a Volvo E Series Excavator (EC300EL) and a D Series Excavator (EC300DL) back-to-back to Blackhead Quarries. Having exposure to both machines really enhanced their decision-making process and enabled them to make an informed choice. They decided to run with the E Series, which also happened to be the first handover of a Volvo machine for their quarry.”



The TDX team are finding numerous other wins associated with the Volvo Demo Zone.

“Having the facility allows us to demonstrate a variety of products in a short amount of time and removes the need to transport large, heavy equipment around the country to sometimes remote and inaccessible areas.

“This not only saves considerable resource, but also helps reduce carbon emissions and improve our sustainability performance.”

A further advantage of the new Volvo Demo Centre is that it is the perfect location to showcase innovative new Volvo technology, such as operating with Hybrid powertrain systems on and off, as well as 2D and 3D GPS systems.

“Thinking outside the square, introducing innovative ideas and embracing new ways to do business, such as the new Volvo Demo Zone, will help maintain TDX as one of the country's leading suppliers of heavy construction and earthmoving equipment,” James.

TDX team member playing a key role in East Coast storm recovery


The recent storms which ravaged the North Island, Hawkes Bay and Gisborne in particular, have affected thousands of people.

TDX Regional Sales Manager Equipment (Central), Karl Scragg, has been playing a key role through his position as Volunteer Chief Fire Officer and Civil Defence Coordinator for the Gisborne (Manutuke) area.

“We were cleaning up after Cyclone Hale and then we were hit by Gabrielle, it's been non-stop since the storm struck on Monday 13 February.
Here in Gisborne we lost communications with the world, there was nothing in and nothing out, we were completely cut off.

“Once we were able to get back in touch, support from the TDX Executive Leadership Team was immediate, they said to get out there and do what you need to be doing.

“My first port of call was to check on our people, ensuring all of our 13 team members in Gisborne, Hawkes Bay and Wairoa were accounted for.

“As soon as access opened up into Gisborne, the TDX Taupo team made their way here bringing the BBQ trailer and food supplies. We've also had great support from Nick Davies who was able to get into Napier.

Once we knew our people were safe, we have been in contact with our customers to see what we could do to help them get back on their feet.

We have been able to repurpose some machinery and also loan equipment such as petrol-powered water blasters to help with the clean up.

“The infrastructure has been absolutely smashed. For example, the main road from Gisborne to Hawkes Bay could be out for 6-12 months, which means the usual 2.5 hour drive is an extended detour and takes 10 hours. It's these sorts of logistics which are going to present significant ongoing challenges.

“I'd like to say a huge thanks to TDX and the Executive Leadership Team, they have been beside us every step of the way.

“It's going to take a heck of a long time before things get back to being anywhere near normal, but it's so heartening to know TDX is standing alongside us, our customers and the wider community.”

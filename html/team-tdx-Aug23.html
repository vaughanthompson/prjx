After a great welcome, driving a digger is next on the list

New CFO Victoria Sergel has been at TDX for three months and has enjoyed a warm and friendly welcome - but there's one thing which she hasn't ticked off just yet…


According to new CFO Victoria Sergel, one thing is for sure about the team at TDX.

“They're a passionate team representing some amazing brands and have been incredibly welcoming to me in the few months I have been part of the business.

“TDX has a genuine family feeling about it, everyone has been so open with sharing knowledge which has helped make my move into the role so enjoyable.”

Based at Head Office in Christchurch, Victoria is looking forward to visiting TDX branches around the country.

“It's important to me to get out and meet the people who make up the TDX team, the people who are the face of our brand and do so much good for our customers and community.”

The recent ActiveCare presentation really impressed Victoria and demonstrated a high level of understanding and engagement within the company whilst also showing how TDX is committed to providing opportunities for customers to benefit.

Something else which she has found impressive is the commitment to the environment and sustainability, which has been highlighted with the Toitu journey that TDX has started on.

“From a CFO's perspective, there are obviously costs involved with such an initiative, but benefits associated with the programme far outweigh money-related considerations. It's encouraging to be part of an organisation that values doing the right thing so highly.”

But there's one thing on Victoria's 'to-do' list which hasn't been ticked off - yet.

“I'd really like to spend some time in the Volvo Demo Zone experiencing some of the amazing machines we have on offer.

“I haven't been in the operator's seat just yet, but the size of them fascinates me - as does how capable they are at doing some really delicate actions.”

“It will be a new and exciting opportunity and who knows, I might be tempted to swap my desk for a new Volvo and get out in the field more often.”

The future is now with new Volvo EC55 electric excavator


Much of the talk around New Zealand in the past year has been about the surge in popularity of electric vehicles. But it's not just cars that are the focus of electrification, there's a growing shift in how e-mobility can benefit other sectors as well.

Here at TDX, via our key partner Volvo, we're about to start a pilot programme with our very first, fully electric excavator - the EC55.

“The new unit has literally just arrived in the country and there are three key areas we're keen to find out about,” said Charles Malcolm, General Manager Equipment Division.

“The first is to learn and understand the machine, to discover how it performs in various conditions.

“Secondly, we want to see what infrastructure is required to support it and the third piece of work relates to educating our customers about the features and benefits of e-mobility.”

The small size of an EC55 means they are nimble and manoeuvrable, and a key benefit of a fully electric machine is that it is silent and there are no emissions. This makes them perfect for urban civil work where there are restrictions around operating times due to noise pollution.

The new EC55 will be used to train and teach customers ahead of the arrival of other Volvo electric commercial models expected at TDX by the end of the year, including the ECR25 mini excavator, L25 compact wheel loader and the L120 wheel loader.

“The e-mobility area, especially in construction, is a rapidly evolving and developing space,” said Charles.

“In the next 3-4 years there are likely to be 20-30 new electric Volvo models released, so it's important from a business growth perspective that we embrace this new technology and learn about it now, so we can hit the ground running when other machines come to market.”

Machine electrification aligns with other projects across the TDX business, such as Toitu decarbonisation and EECA-related initiatives.

“We're eager and hungry to learn about these innovative machines, the more we know and understand them then the more we can help our customers,” added Charles.

“It's all about developing and growing the TDX experience, keeping our customers at the heart of everything we do, delivering a world-class experience combined with local know-how.”

EECA working with TDX on our decarbonisation journey


EECA is the Energy Efficiency and Conservation Authority, a government agency that is responsible for promoting energy efficiency, energy conservation and the use of renewable energy sources.


In addition to providing insulation and heater grants to low-income homeowners to create warm, dry, healthy homes, EECA provides co-investment and support for businesses and the public sector to accelerate decarbonisation.

General Manager - People and Safety, Alle McGrath, is part of the TDX EECA project team which also includes Colm Hamrogue, Charles Malcolm, Andrew Falloon, James O'Çonnor, Mark Falloon and Vee MacLeod.

TDX recently submitted an application to ECCA for a Low Emission Transport project, a funding round that focuses on off-road vehicles that move goods.

“I'm thrilled to reveal we were successful with this particular application and will receive financial support to conduct the project,” said Alle.

“The transport sector currently produces over 50% of NZ's CO2 emissions, so it's an area in which we can help make a positive contribution.”

The application had to demonstrate innovative solutions, potential for future adoption and deployment, emission reduction, address the market and organisational barriers, and share learnings and knowledge to stimulate wider replication of successful projects and solutions.

“We saw the opportunity to introduce Volvo electric loaders into New Zealand as part of the project,” said Alle.


“To assist with this, we also applied for funding for two mobile charging units that travel with the loaders and the installation of a charging station in the Hamilton and Christchurch branch.”

    We will demonstrate three electric wheel loaders:
  1. Pre-production Volvo JL120 20-tonne electric wheel loader
  2. Volvo L25 electric wheel loader leased to customer
  3. Volvo L25 electric wheel loader for extended test-drives with multiple customers Nationwide
    The project is successful if it:
  1. Shows electric construction machinery meets real-world operational requirements.
  2. Establishes the leasing business model for electric construction equipment.
  3. Results in NZ becoming a regular proving ground for Volvo, giving early access to new models.
  4. Increases demand for electric construction equipment procurement.

“A compelling reason why we were successful in our application is that we're taking action at a national level, it's not just limited to one location,” mentioned Alle.

“It's a great opportunity for positive collaboration, as EECA is helping and supporting TDX to become more sustainable and we're demonstrating efficient carbon reduction solutions to our customers.”

Other EECA Key co-investment opportunities have included:
NZ Post - From fuel to Electric Vans
McCain - From coal pre-heaters to pulsed electric field technology
Fonterra - Conversion of a 43 megawatt coal boiler to use wood pellets at Fonterra's Te Awamutu dairy factory
Wellington Harbour - The Southern Hemispheres first all-electric ferry

Hero Staff Member - Jono Clerke




Jono's rising through the ranks at TDX

12 years at TDX has seen Jono Clerke progress through a number of roles into the position he holds today and, according to General Manager Aftersales Operations Joe Gradwell, he is an example of what can be achieved with hard work and dedication.

“Jono started as an apprentice in our Taupo branch just over 12 years ago and then relocated once the Napier branch was established,” said Joe.

“You could tell he was someone who was committed and keen to learn. Since that time, he moved to Technician and now is the Foreman at Napier branch, a position he made his own when the new location opened.

“He's built a rock-solid team around him and leads by example, particularly demonstrating TDX values which focus on the customer.”

Another of Jono's key attributes is outstanding product knowledge.

“When our customers come to the branch with a specific need or requirement, Jono is usually the man with the answer,” said Joe.

“He's a great man doing well and is an awesome person to have as part of the TDX team. We thank Jono for all that he does to support our team and also our customers.

“I would also like to congratulate Jono and his partner Hollie on the recent arrival of their new son, Parker Clerke.”

This month's introduction is from Richard Papps, National Sales Manager - Yanmar.


The Yanmar range of mini excavators is painting NZ premium red as the machines become an increasingly familiar sight around the country. With the latest Financial Year coming to a close at the end of March, we thought it a good opportunity for Richard Papps, National Sales Manager for Yanmar, to share some thoughts about the previous 12-months.

Yanmar is a growing and integral part of the TDX portfolio, the machines we supply offer specific features and benefits which appeal to smaller and medium owner/ operators and businesses such as hire outlets.

We've enjoyed steady and sustained growth since 2020, but it's fair to say the previous 12- months have been a very different market.

There have been a number of well documented headwinds and distractions, some of which include the October election, increasing cost of living as well as uncertainty around rising interest rates. These factors have all played a role influencing buying behaviour which impacts demand.

But with every challenge is an opportunity. We're dynamic and nimble and have been able to pivot from one customer base to another. For example, hire companies have reduced their buying due to young fleets and higher interest rates, so we're playing in other areas, such as end users in the construction market.

These conditions also inspired us to look at doing things differently, as the saying goes, 'necessity is the mother of invention.'

With a bit of help from our friends in marketing, we launched a number of promotions which achieved results which exceeded our expectations.

The results generated by these campaigns helped us to increase our market share in a decreasing market and also pick up additional sales within the construction market.

These impressive achievements saw Yanmar finish the year as Number One brand for compact machinery in New Zealand, a result that Yanmar are very proud of.

There's also much to look forward to.

At the end of May we've been invited to attend Sales and Service training in Japan for the release of the new ViO35-7. This is the first model release on the new Dash 7 and the first new model Yanmar have released in quite some time, so it will be great to have some new features to offer to the market.

In summary, while it was a tougher year at the coal face, it was still a very good one.

I want to thank everyone who has been part of achieving this solid result and the roles they play in making it happen.

It takes the full package, from the sales team to the order desk to process orders, the PD team to get machines ready, service and parts to back the machine up and the warehouse and central services to back us all up.

It's a sensational team effort and one we should all be very proud of, thank you for your contributions!

Ceres Demolition

TDX has just delivered two new Volvo machines to Ceres New Zealand, a general contracting firm specialising in the planning and execution of large-scale, complex construction, disaster recovery, demolition and deconstruction projects.

Andrew Falloon, Regional Sales Manager Equipment Southern, provides some behind-the-scenes insight into this special project.


Ceres first approached TDX in 2021 and asked us to supply equipment to assist their demolition business.

Over a number of months we trialled a variety of solutions. The machines we provided could do most of what they needed - but not everything. It became clear we were going to need to come up with something special to tick all of their boxes.

We had many talks with Ceres about exactly what they needed, we wanted to understand their exact requirements and develop a fit-for-purpose solution.

A few key considerations were they needed a machine that could extend 28-metres high and be capable of operating a 2.6-tonne attachment at the end of the boom. They also needed it to be able to enter a building horizontally, to get into and through a demo site to a specific area. They also wanted to be able to swap between boom systems quickly and effortlessly when out on a job site.

After consultation with Ceres, we figured the best solution would be to develop a three- armed articulated boom system.

We drew up some plans and then, using a Volvo EC480D Crawler Excavator as the base, started working with Volvo and their partner, Sun and Shield in Korea, to develop the machine.

Six months later, we finally received the boom kits from Sun and Shield, it was several containers full of dozens of boxes of equipment for the new machine. It was a massive jigsaw puzzle and the team had to decipher and assemble all of the components, it took almost three months to put together.

We got there in the end, the final result is a machine which is incredibly functional and versatile, looks impressive and is a phenomenal piece of equipment.



While there have been a number of people involved in the project, I'd like to make special mention of Keiran Lindsay, Matt Miller and Matt Holloway. The entire team maintained TDX values at all times, especially putting the customer first and working with them to deliver a solution that exceeds their expectations.

Everyone involved in the project is stoked with the final product, customer feedback is absolutely positive and we can't wait to get it into action.

The machinery is unique, there is no other Volvo equipment like it in New Zealand, and the end result is a very happy customer.


 

Finance is going to party like it's EOFY Eve!


Not many people in TDX get to celebrate two New Year Eves, but for Chief Financial Officer Victoria Sergel and her team, that's exactly what they get to do….

The countdown to midnight on December 31 is usually 'the big night' for most TDX'ers, but all of the Finance team have a second special end-of-the-year date to look forward to - March 31.

“The End of the Financial Year is a massive milestone for the business, it marks the close of one accounting period and the start of an incredibly busy month when we prepare reports for stakeholders such as banks, shareholders and insurance requirements,” said TDX CFO, Victoria Sergel.

“April is when we sit down and ensure all financials are present and accounted for before creating external statements to provide a complete picture of business performance over the previous year.”

The 2023 Financial year has been a big one for the team.

“I started at TDX in May and, together with the Management Team, have been involved with making changes to how Finance operates and interacts with business units. “We reorganised the team through September and October, redefining roles and responsibilities, to better align people and their skills with business needs.

“The outcome is that Finance team members now work much more closely with operational managers, which is paying dividends for TDX.”

New budgeting templates and financial reporting statements have also been introduced.

“These help give operational staff a better view of their areas so they can manage costs, as well as make the most of business opportunities,” said Victoria.

“It also means our systems and processes are more streamlined which makes reporting for the EOFY more straightforward - team leaders have a better idea of what's needed to help us put together our reports.

“We look forward to providing a snapshot of our performance when everything is wrapped up, then counting down the days to the next New Years Eve!”

Hero Staff Member:
Dylan Bennet,
TDX Christchurch


Dylan Bennet, a member of the TDX Rebuild Centre team in Christchurch, recently had the opportunity to work for an extended time on the West Coast. During his three months based at the Greymouth office he worked on a variety of Volvo machinery, which provided invaluable experience for his next steps in his TDX journey. His story is told by Scott Moodie, Operations Manager - Upper South Island.


Dylan started with TDX in September 2022 as a Service Technician and came from a Heavy Road transport background. He quickly proved his worth as part of the Rebuild Team repairing and rebuilding a variety of components.

There was a recent opportunity to assist the TDX Greymouth team on the West Coast for a month which turned into three, to help them deal with an increasing workload. Dylan stepped up and grabbed the chance to pop over to the other side of the Alps to fill the gap and became part of the crew working on Volvo machinery in and around the mines.

It was a great chance to learn on the job and, after getting inducted, gave him first-hand insight into machinery applications - where they operate and the type of work they do.

There's nothing like real life experience for knowledge advancement and this 'learning through doing' has meant he's been able to almost complete Level Four Heavy Plant and Equipment qualification.

Dan Watson, TDX Service Manager at Greymouth and who had Dylan as part of his operation, told me many good things about Dylan - that he quickly became an integral part of the operation, is reliable, quick on the tools, keen to learn, gets stuck in and does a great job.

He's also good with customers, he takes the time to listen to them and understand a problem before developing and delivering a solution.




Since returning from the Coast, he's sustained a bit of an injury which has meant he's not able to be on the tools. Rather than sit about, he's getting involved in some training of young apprentices, passing on knowledge and paying it forward to the next bunch of TDX techs coming through the ranks.

Dylan is an example of what a TDX team member is all about - he has a great work ethic, is customer focused and is willing to share his knowledge and expertise with new apprentices. All things considered, he has the hallmarks of an outstanding TDX Service Technician and is someone who is willing to step up and make the most of opportunities.

Thanks for all of your hard work, Dylan - it's certainly been noticed and is much appreciated.

Introduction by Greg Slaughter, CEO.



Kia ora team,

It's been fantastic getting out and meeting so many of you across the TDX and LDX teams recently.

Visiting our North Island branches last week, I started my road trip with an early morning BBQ of eggs and black pudding by the awesome Whangarei team.

The engagement and hospitality only got better from there as I moved down the motu. We really do have a dedicated bunch of people who make this company what it is — thank you for the energy, teamwork, and commitment you continue to bring every day.



A special thanks to our Parts, Service and Consumables teams, who tracked above EBIT budget last month, and to PPD for their consistent contribution. These results don't happen by chance — they're the outcome of hard work, focus, and doing the basics brilliantly.

While it's still a tough market out there, there are encouraging signs of growth. We've got a number of large tenders on the table, and our sales teams across the country are putting in great work to secure these opportunities.

As we head into the final stretch of the year, our key priorities remain clear:
  • Safety first - Take 5 before starting any new task and speak up if you feel unsafe.
  • Continue cost-saving initiatives, taking time with billing, minimise WIP and focus on efficiencies.
  • Keep energy and optimism high in pursuing sales opportunities.
  • Most importantly, take care of our customers — let's make sure every interaction reflects the world-class experience that defines TDX and LDX.

Looking ahead, the next month will bring several key activities and updates across the business — from ongoing tender work to end-of-year planning and customer engagement events. It's an exciting time as we close out what's been a big year for both TDX and LDX.


Thanks again for all your efforts
— keep up the great work!
Greg

 

Turning Defects into Opportunities


Spotting and recording machine issues might sound routine, but for the Lower South Island team, the Defect identification process has become a real game-changer. It no longer relates only to safety, but for business growth too.


A Defect list forms part of the service check sheet and identifies and describes any flaws or issues found in a machine during servicing or repair work.

“It's primarily about safety and ongoing machine reliability,” says Daryl Harper, Operations Manager for the Lower South Island. “But it's also presenting a growing revenue opportunity for TDX.”

This starts on site when a technician identifies a defect during a service or repair. Issues are detailed on the service check sheet, often with photos attached.

“After every job, we send out the check sheet to the customer,” explains Daryl. “If a safety-related issue is identified, it's raised immediately on site with the customer.

“The service check sheets are sent with an autogenerated email to the customer, however we had realised those emails weren't always being followed up by the customer and potentially end up in the wrong department, so now we take a more personalised approach,” Daryl says.

“Our Service Advisor, Morag Thorne, manages the defects for the Lower South Island and contacts our customers directly, which has proven to be much more effective.”

And the results speak for themselves.

“In August alone, we did work on three wheel loaders for Ballance, all jobs that came directly from defect reports. That added up to around $70K in additional revenue to the region,” says Daryl.

Just as importantly, technicians are sharpening their skills and looking out for potential issues before they become problems.

“Our service team is giving customers real peace of mind,” Daryl adds.

“They know TDX has their back, keeping them safe and their machines in top shape.”

 

C3 and TDX - A Partnership Built on Trust and Knowhow


If you've spent any time around the ports, you've probably seen C3's name pop up. They're one of New Zealand's biggest players in forestry logistics and are a key customer for TDX. Headquartered in Tauranga, C3 operates at most of the country's major ports, handling everything from logs to containers.

They run their fleet with machines from TDX and it's a mixed bag of serious gear, from Volvo equipment through to Sennebogen material handlers. With more than 80 machines running across New Zealand, keeping everything in top shape is no small task. Most of these workhorses clock up around 45,000 hours in their lifetime, so C3 in conjunction with TDX has developed a smart “mid-life rebuild” programme to get the best bang for their buck.

At around 15,000 hours, the big machines come in for a full eight-week refresh. Depending on the site, C3 might handle the rebuild themselves. However, when they don't have workshop support, they call in the TDX team. That's where our knowhow and manpower really shine. We've been trusted to take on full rebuilds, supply key parts, and provide skilled labour wherever it's needed.

About seven machines go through the rebuild process each year, giving us plenty of opportunities to work closely with the C3 crew. The partnership isn't just about machines, it's about people, collaboration, and trust built over years of doing things right.





C3 has become one of our strongest advocates, regularly singing TDX's praises and recommending us across their network. It's the kind of endorsement money can't buy and is a great example of what happens when we go the extra mile for our customers.

Here's to keeping the partnership rolling strong for years to come!
 

TDX Tauranga - Small Team, Big Impact


Sitting right in the heart of Mt Maunganui, just a stone's throw from the Port of Tauranga, the TDX Tauranga branch is proof that great things really do come in small packages. With a team of just 11, the branch punches well above its weight when it comes to productivity, customer relationships and financial contribution to the business.


“Our location's a big advantage,” says Craig Wymer, National Aftersales Manager, Tauranga. “Many of our key customers, like C3 and Qube, operate around the port, so we're right in the thick of it. It means we can respond quickly and build strong, hands-on relationships.”

Much of the branch's work revolves around supporting customers who use wheel loaders, Material Handlers and excavators for log handling, stock feed, and fertiliser operations. “These machines are working hard every day,” Craig explains, “so keeping them running smoothly is critical. That's where our team really shines.”

It's not just the results that make Tauranga special — it's the people. The branch has a solid mix of experience and longevity, with most of the crew having been with TDX for several years. “We've got a great bunch here,” Craig says.

“There is a good spread of ages in the team, we get on really well, and we've built a strong culture where everyone has each other's backs.”

Outside of work, the team keeps the good vibes rolling with an active social club and plenty of local events. “We like to have a bit of fun,” Craig adds. “It keeps morale high and makes this a place people enjoy coming to every day.”

A small team with big energy, TDX Tauranga continues to set the bar higher — and they're showing no signs of slowing down.

Hero Staff Member - Vee MacLeod


In this month's Hero a Staff Member, the person featured is well respected, highly regarded and, if you look up 'team player' in the dictionary, there is a photo of Vee. We spoke with Joe, John and Andrew to find out what makes Vee such a standout member of the team.

“Vee joined the business three years ago, initially starting out in TDX and then moving across to LDX when we set up the new operation,” said Joe.

“She is an absolute superstar and is passionate about helping everyone - not just the people in her team, but anyone in the business.

“One of her overwhelming endearing qualities is that she makes time to care.”

It's this level of care which extends to our customers as well. For example, she is very influential in the Fonterra partnership, she has an eagle eye on the deliverables and her involvement with the relationship means they receive the absolute best care and attention.

“She is also an absolute whizz when it comes to Excel and spreadsheets, her expertise in this area means there are systems and processes in place which save considerable time, energy and resources.”

John chimed in with similar sentiments.

“Vee is like a camp mother of sorts, she is very caring, empathetic and nurturing. She has the best interests of the team at heart and always leads by example, nothing is ever a problem for her.

“She is someone who lives and breathes the values we have at work, stepping them up and adding her own personal touch. She's simply an all-round great person.

From a general operations perspective, Andrew has had a lot to do with Vee.

“I have found Vee to be integral to implementation of systems and processes in the LDX business.

“Her Excel skills are legendary and she offers help right across the board. She manages projects and never rests until a job is completed.

“When it comes to Fonterra, she certainly has her finger on the pulse and we have every confidence knowing that Vee is in charge and has a situation under control.”

And perhaps Andrew summed up Vee's impact on the business best...

“She's an absolute legend, she saves my arse and helps me big time when I get into technical issues.

“She's like my little helper, my angel even, we'd be absolutely lost without her.”

Top work, Vee - thanks for everything you do for the team!

Lee Jenkins, Director.


Mark Keatley sat down with Lee Jenkins, who has recently become a director of the company.

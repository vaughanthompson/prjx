A personal reflection on Workplace Safety - Greg Campbell, TDX Chair


This issue of the TDX Newsletter starts with a safety message from our Chairman of the Board, Greg Campbell.

Over my career as a CEO and Director in many different industry sectors, I've had the unfortunate duty of facing families, friends and colleagues of employees who have lost their lives or been severely injured while on the job. These tragic incidents leave a lasting impact - an immediate void in the lives of loved ones and a long-term loss for our community. In every instance, these losses were avoidable. They often stemmed from staff attempting to help by taking on tasks beyond the initial scope, underestimating the risks involved.

One of the hardest things I've ever had to do is stand before a grieving family and trying to offer some semblance of comfort, knowing that their loss could have been prevented. These experiences have taught me that we must never assume, “It won't happen here”, or “It won't happen to us”. Safety should always be our top priority.

As you go about your work, I urge you to look after yourself and your colleagues, you wouldn't live with yourself if you saw a colleague undertaking a task with uncontrolled risk and you never spoke up. If at any point for any reason you feel uncomfortable or unsure about a task, speak up. Ask yourself this critical question; Would I be comfortable with my most loved one doing this task as I am? If the answer is no, then it is time to stop and reassess.

Take care out there. Let's ensure that everyone goes home safely at the end of each day.

All this activity can be summarized in one award:

“TDX Dealer of the year for Volvo CE Region International”.


Congratulations again to all the TDX Team, be proud and please keep-up your efforts and well done.

Take care,

Inside: TDX National Warehousing


When you think of a logistical challenge, one thing that springs to mind is getting the right part or product from the correct TDX warehouse to any one of the 18 branches around the country - then doing it hundreds of times a day.


Phil Bracefield, General Manager - Supply Chain (pictured), is the person charged with overseeing the process, while Dean McKenzie, National Warehouse & Logistics Manager, looks after the implementation side. Starting with Phil, this story looks into the 'Why' and the 'How' of the operation.

“We've been looking at ways to enhance the supply chain process, as this ultimately benefits our customers and the experience they have with TDX.

“Over the past 10 months we have been focusing on the resources, the capability and also the processes, with my main focus on where we can make improvements to deliver an even better experience.

“We benchmarked existing processes against best practice as it's important to know where our baseline performance is at, to help focus our goal of providing a world-class experience.

“Identifying knowledge gaps and areas for improvement means we can keep developing and refining how we operate, which is a win-win for all involved - from TDX thorough to our customers.

“One thing we're wanting to implement is better freight forwarding visibility, as this gives us better control of what is going where and when.

“We are committed to drive up the level of service and processes out of the warehouse and were fortunate to bring Dean into the business as National Warehouse Logistics Manager.

“Thanks to his experience, he's been able to hit the ground running and we're already achieving some outstanding results and are working towards our goal of delivering an outstanding logistics experience.”

We'll now hear from Dean, who has just finished his first month with TDX.

“I have worked with Phil in a previous role, so we know each other well - we have complementary styles and understand what's needed to make real gains in warehousing and supply chain. I also have a post-grad degree in logistics from Massey and look forward to bringing these skills to the job.

“It's been a busy first four weeks, coming to grips with and understanding the size of the task ahead of us.

“TDX is a very impressive business and is partnered with some tremendously powerful brands, so it's on us to maintain the quality and standing of these brands, delivering the expected level of service at the right cost.

“We need to ensure there are replacement parts in stock and that they are readily available, so a good understanding of the supply chain is vital.

“There are a number of challenges associated with this, such as the geography of the country as well as the stretch of water between the two islands. These factors can make getting parts out to all 18 branches difficult, but it's important to get it right to help maintain trust, credibility and reputation of TDX and the brands we represent.

“Another unique set of challenges is the size and variations of parts and consumables we carry, as well as significant lead times associated with sourcing items from offshore - whether they are roll on-roll off arrivals, container loads of equipment or fast moving consumer goods like Shell Oil products. We cater to a really diverse and interesting range of requirements.

“We're under no illusions there is a lot of work to be done and it's a long road ahead, but I'm confident we're heading in the right direction.”
 

National Quarry Conference / A New Yanmar


The Marketing and sales teams have been involved in two major events recently, the National Quarry Conference and the launch of a new Yanmar model. Here's a report on each event.

The National Quarry Conference is considered one of the most prestigious annual events in the country. This year it was held in Rotorua in mid-July and TDX was there, together with Shell and Volvo CE.

Having a presence at the event is important, as it demonstrates that TDX is serious about supporting those who support us. It's also a great way for us to connect with new and existing customers.

A highlight of day one, was James O'Connor giving an overview of the EC500 which forms part of the new series of Volvo Excavators which we are launching this year. He also gave an overview of the Volvo Electric JL120 pilot loader which has been in test with FH from late last year. A video of James giving an overview on each machine can be viewed below:



On the Friday morning Colm and Wayne Richardson, Manufacturing Product & Sales Manager, Fulton Hogan gave a keynote talk about Volvo's Electric machine ambitions and about how the JL120 pilot has gone so far from FH's perspective.

The conference finished on Friday night with the TDX/Volvo awards night, which recognises individuals and companies within the industry.

TDX has been a loyal supporter of the awards night for 15 years, which has to be some sort of record in the industry.

Having this sort of long-standing relationship shows shared commitment and also a healthy mutual respect between TDX and quarry customers.

There are a few challenges out there and it's not all roses in the market at the moment, so it's even more important that we stand by those who support us.

A standout from the night was Nigel's award, this is outstanding recognition of his efforts dealing with people and the support he's giving the industry. He's done a monumental job and thoroughly deserves the accolade.

Thank you to the wider TDX team who have been involved in organising and attend and support what is a key industry event.


The Yanmar 35-7 Launch

There's nothing like a new model launch to get people to sit/ up and take interest! For the first time in six years, Yanmar had a new machine to showcase.

The TDX team launched the all-new Yanmar 35-7 and 30-7 excavator range on July 25 at an event in Auckland.

50 Yanmar customers were invited to the event and were well impressed by the new model. The new 35-7 was on display for customers to explore, while all customers also received merchandise, information packs and exclusive pricing for the current range of Yanmar machines.

Feedback from the customers was very positive, so much so that a few deals were done on the night and a couple more the next day, with the anticipation of a lot more enquiry.

One thing that was evident on the night is that Yanmar has a strong following in New Zealand. All customers spoke highly of the brand and the performance of the machines, especially their reliability, and also the positive relationship they have with the TDX team.

As more machines land over the coming months, the marketing team will continue to push the 35-7. The next opportunity to feature it is at the Civil Contractors Conference which is on this month in Invercargill, as well as in a series of print and digital advertising campaigns.

 

CrowdStrike Outage


Did the 'Blue Screen of Death' impact TDX during the recent CrowdStrike outage?

Hitting late on a Friday afternoon at a time when people start thinking about their weekends, our IT team swung into action to avert the impending catastrophe. Here's some information about the great work the team did to manage the situation.

What is CrowdStrike and what's this all about?


CrowdStrike is an American cybersecurity technology company that provides cloud-based security solutions for various platforms. It also manages and responds to threats as well as provides cyber attack response services.

On Friday July 19, a bug in CrowdStrike's cloud-based testing system - specifically, the part that runs validation checks on new updates prior to release - ended up allowing the software to be pushed out despite containing 'problematic content data.'

In short, the global IT outage came down to a single software update. The update to the Falcon sensor programme, run by CrowdStrike, caused a coding error that sent millions of Windows computers worldwide to a 'Blue Screen of Death.'

The outage affected numerous Windows PC and computer systems worldwide, effectively shutting down many businesses and causing hundreds of millions of dollars of losses globally.

What happened at TDX?

TDX first began to experience the issue at 5pm when multiple alerts were received when all 33 production servers started to reboot.

Impact

To resolve the reboot issue, the decision was made to remove the Falcon sensors from our production servers. This was to prevent damage to the underlying operating system from multiple crashes and to ensure the environment was operational for Saturday trading.

CrowdStrike released an update file to resolve the issue at 5.27pm but, by this time, TDX IT had resolved the reboot issue on 80% of the production servers. IT continued to monitor CrowdStrike updates in case there were any other issues introduced through the correct file update.

Resolution

No tickets were raised from the network during Saturday trading indicating the actions taken Friday evening minimised the impact on TDX operations.

On Sunday, the Falcon sensor agent was re-installed on all production servers. The servers were monitored to ensure no issues occurred.

Final Outcome

A communication was sent to all TDX staff providing an update on the weekend's events and providing instructions on the steps required if their local device experienced the Blue Screen issue. Only 11 users reported issues with their local device, of which 10 have been remediated with one outstanding.

We should recognise the outstanding effort the IT team went to managing this issue and also the extra work they did ensuring systems and processes continued to run smoothly during this difficult time, great work by everyone involved!

Hero Staff Member:
Nigel Maxwell


When he's not out riding dirt bikes, TDX staff member Nigel Maxwell can be found hard at work in and around the Nelson area in his role as a Field Service Technician.
We spoke with Scott Moodie who gave us a bit of a glimpse into Nigel and his work as part of this month's Hero a Staff Member section.


Nigel has been with TDX three and a half years, he's really excelled as a Field Service technician. His official base is Nelson, but in reality, he could be out and about throughout the upper South Island.

Most recently, he's been doing two week shifts at the Stockton Mine. He jumped at the chance to be involved and has used it as a real learning experience, expanding his knowledge and expertise around mining equipment.

He's skilled at what he does - he's efficient , loves getting the job done and doesn't mind getting his hands dirty. He's also more often than not the first to put his hand up when a new challenge presents itself whether this be Whalewatch Vessels in Kaikoura or Power generation for off grid properties in the Motueka Valley.

Nigel originally qualified in Heavy Road Transport but after starting with TDX he took the opportunity to work towards the NZ Certificate of Automotive Engineering Plant and Equipment which he achieved recently. This was a great achievement and shows his commitment to our industry.

This recent qualification demonstrates he has the skills and knowledge to further himself at TDX, he's also a real team player and instils his work ethics in our apprentices. He has TDX values at the heart of everything he does, especially when it comes to putting the customer first.





Congratulations Nigel on this recognition, we applaud your work ethic and values - time to get out on the bike!

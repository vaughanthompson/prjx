Connecting with the right people at the right time in the right way.

This month's introduction is from Marc Warr, TDX Marketing Manager, who will provide some insights into the world of Marketing and detail the strategy behind attending various events over the coming months.


There's more to Marketing than producing a few ads to go in industry publications, there's a much bigger strategy at play when it comes to what we do, where and when.

Marketing could be considered as being responsible for laying the building blocks to set us up for success.

In consultation with other business units, we determine the best way to gain visibility and raise awareness of TDX and the partners we represent, to generate sales and ongoing business.

It's all about connecting with the right people at the right time in the right way, which ultimately helps build market share. Our partners have a huge variety of impressive products coming, there's no better way to show them off than being involved in key events.

And, there's a lot for us to talk about on the product front so that's why you will find TDX at all of these events, it's going to be a hectic six months:

  • Volvo Days - 2 - 8 June
    TDX will be hosting 10 key Volvo customers over in Sweden. They will be learning about the next generation of Volvo CE and visiting a number of key Volvo factories. It's great to have the event back running with the last event being run in 2018 and a key opportunity to continue to strengthen our customer relationships and network with Volvo and other key Volvo dealers

  • National Fieldays - 12 - 15 June, Mystery Creek
    Huge sales event with over 130,000 people attending. This year our partners from Volvo, Yanmar and Linde will be joining the TDX team onsite

  • National Quarry Conference - 17-19 July, Rotorua
    Over 500 quarry and mining delegates, will feature the TDX presentations on the Volvo Electric JL120 loader, new generation (F Series) range. The event will finish in style with the Volvo/TDX awards dinner

  • Hirecon Conference - 23-24 July, Auckland
    Opportunity to showcase the new Yanmar 35-7 to the hire market

  • CCNZ National Conference - 14 - 17 august, Invercargill
    Opportunity to showcase the new Yanmar 35-7 and electric Volvo Compact excavator range to the contractor's market

  • Annual Cold Storage Conference - 11-13 August, Queenstown
    Opportunity for the Linde team to display a range of Linde products and network with the industry

On top of all these events, we also have a number of new product launches, including:

  • Electric Volvo Compact excavator range
  • Next generation (F Series) Volvo range
  • All-new Yanmar 35-7 compact excavator, the first launch of Yanmar machines in recent years
  • Shell Panolin, High Fi Filtration
  • Leveraging the Lithium Ion Linde products
  • Continued rollout of Volvo connected and productivity services

We have never had such a great opportunity to talk about exciting new products!

The marketing team feeds of local content and we're also keen to promote stories from our people and customers, please continue to share any stories you may have so we inturn can share and keep building on the TDX story.

We are entering an exciting and busy period from a marketing perspective. It's a challenging market with every company and brand scrapping for share, but we're in a great position to make the most of every opportunity.

One of the country's leading forest products companies, Carter Holt Harvey, recently took delivery of four new Volvo loaders. Here's a little 'behind the scenes look' into this particular partnership, one that's been two years in the making.
Stephane Bessette - Territory Manager, Auckland

The process started in early May 2022, we were approached by the Head of Procurement from CHH and asked to put forward a proposal for four new Volvo Loaders.

Before we started anything, we had to find out more about their needs and wants, things like where the machinery was going to be used, whether they wanted servicing bundled as part of the package and if they wanted any finance options. They also wanted to know more about TDX and what we were all about.

One advantage that TDX has is a nationwide coverage, this was especially helpful as CHH wanted two machines in Nelson and two in Kinleith near Tokoroa. We have reps in both of these locations, Trevor and Ewen, so they were able to get out into the field to meet with operators and find out even more about their requirements.

We were able to build trust and confidence across the CHH business. It's this sort of collaborative team approach which is needed to win the deal on the ground.

While all of this was happening, we were continuing to engage with the people in Head Office, in fact eventually with the Chief Financial Officer in Australia. This was another thing that CHH enjoyed about TDX, is that we have team members who can be involved across all aspects of a project and can work collaboratively to find the best possible solution for our customers.

The deal was signed in August 2023, more than one year after discussions got underway. The fourth and final machine is about to be delivered, which goes to show how much time and effort is invested in making these types of deals happen - literally just over two years.

It is a significant deal for TDX consisting of four machines and four service agreements, one that has involved many different people across our business.

Key for me out of all of this is the two-way feedback throughout the organisation and the ability to deal with a wide range of different people at CHH, from workers on ground to C- Suite Executives and everyone in between. We should all be really proud of making this deal happen, it's been an absolute team effort.

Trevor Newport - Territory Manager: Nelson / Marlborough / West Coast

I'm based in Nelson so I was responsible for visiting some of the nearby CHH operations in our area and making preliminary assessments of their sites.

We recommended two machines - a L220H and a L150H. One is a primary truck unloader which feeds the mill itself, the other machine is a Utility Loader, which can operate with a bucket for loading wood chips into trucks.

One of the really exciting things is the feedback from the operators, they really love the machines - even more than the Cats they replaced. They especially like the comfort and layout of the cabin.

An unexpected bonus is that they have found they are able to stack the wood even higher at the yard, giving them more room in the log area, meaning it's a lot easier for general operation.

I have to agree with Stephane, this has been a real team effort and is one example of how TDX shines above so many others out there in this space, our values are held in high regard and people enjoy doing business with us.


Ewen Satherley - Territory Manager Volvo / Forestry Specialist Bay of Plenty

One of my roles in the CHH deal was getting in touch with the crew working at the Kinleith Mill, just south of Tokoroa.

Nothing beats time on the ground and at the site, talking with the workers and finding out what they need to get the job done.

Once I had an idea of their requirements, I arranged for them to visit another contractor who uses a Volvo machine. It meant the team was able to see it in action, drive and experience it as well as talk with the operator and find out how it really performed.

The crew enjoyed the opportunity and were really happy with the experience, it was invaluable to put our money where our mouth was and show them the product hard at work.

Time spent with the guys also meant we were able to build a relationship and show them the trust and credibility built up between TDX and our customers.

The end result is two Volvo L120H machines are now working at the plant. The tasks include discharging trucks, loading the mill and sorting out stock in the yard.

As with the Nelson experience, the feedback has been outstanding. Compared with their previous machines, the new Volvos use less fuel and are quieter, more productive as they can carry greater volume inside the head (1.3m to 1.7m), are more comfortable and nicer to drive.

It's been an outstanding team effort all round!

Hero a Staff Member- Jordan Wilson


Jordan Wilson is a member of the TDX Invercargill Parts team and has recently stepped up to help his TDX Dunedin Parts colleagues. Here's his story, as told by Daryl Harper (Operations Manager, Lower South Island), as to why Jordan is this month's TDX Hero.


Jordan has been with TDX for just over two years, he came from a similar parts role background involving motorcycle parts - not heavy machinery. It's naturally been a bit of a change, but he's taken to his role like a duck to water and has made a really positive impression around the place.

Recently, there was a need for the Invercargill team to help out the Dunedin branch with some on-the-ground support. Jordan stepped into the gap where and when we needed him, he didn't hesitate to put himself forward to get the job done.

Part of working in Dunedin means that he has had to travel away from home since the end of February and often for extended periods. He's handled this disruption well and has similarly impressed customers at the Dunedin location.

Jordan has great qualities and attributes - his work ethic is impressive, he's always willing to help and go the extra mile, nothing is ever an issue and he sticks around to get a job done.



Like many 'Hero a Staff Member' nominees, Jordan is someone who has the TDX values front of mind and refers back to them as part of his day-to-day operation.

I definitely appreciate all he does and he certainly deserves the gratitude he receives as part of this recognition. His willingness to step up demonstrates he lives the TDX Values and I'm excited about the future he has here.

Above all else, he always has a smile on his face and can be considered an all-round bloody legend.

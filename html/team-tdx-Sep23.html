Six months on, resilience and perseverance shining through in Gisborne


This month's introduction is from Craig Wymer, Operations Manager - Central North Island, who is providing some observations 'from over the range' in Tauranga about the Gisborne region following Cyclone Gabrielle in mid-February.


Valentine's Day is usually an opportunity for catching up with someone special, but February 14 this year will be remembered by the people of Gisborne in particular with anything but loving fondness.

It signalled the start of a week with no telecommunications, when Gisborne and its surrounds were literally cut off by roads damaged beyond repair.

“It's often in times of adversity that people show their true colours, and watching from afar it was clear to see that the small team at TDX are a bunch of top-drawer characters who developed solutions for the situation they found themselves in,” said Craig.

“It's fair to say the Gisborne area was in a massive state of flux, but the TDX branch swung into action and were amongst the first to get involved helping their local communities.

“For our guys, this was their primary focus and consisted of cooking BBQ's, dropping off care packages and helping people get back on their feet through clean-ups.

“TDX Management should be recognised here as well, as there was no pressure or expectation of resuming 'business as usual' until the guys on the ground were in a position to re-engage properly.”

The Gisborne branch received outstanding support from branches in the CNI region and TDX Management as a whole.

“When the time was right, they swung right back into getting on with business,” said Craig.

“This was the perfect time to demonstrate the 'locals helping locals' philosophy, it wasn't about pumping out the product - it was making sure the right stuff was getting to where it was needed most. The guys rolled up their sleeves to keep customers going in the region.

“Everyone at TDX should be proud knowing our team in the region, went above and beyond and continue to lead by example.”

Things are slowly returning to pre-cyclone levels of activity but, even today, there are still hold ups and slips and challenges to life throughout the region.

“It's going to take a while to get back to 'normal,' but if there's one lesson out of everything it's that TDX isn't afraid to get involved and share in the heavy lifting - and that speaks volumes about our business and people,” added Craig.
 

Impressive mileage and an impressive legacy after more than 22 years at TDX


When you think about Rotorua, the first things that often spring to mind are bubbling hot pools, geysers, gondolas and, from a TDX perspective, Gary Sanders.


Gary has been part of the team since January 2001, but this month on the 22nd of September he will set out on his next adventure - retirement.

“I started as a Territory Sales Rep, always based in Rotorua, and covered the area between Hamilton, south to Palmerston North, west to New Plymouth and everywhere in between.

“In the 20 years of doing that role, I reckon I must have covered more than one million kilometres. I loved being on the road, I really enjoyed meeting the customers and helping them find fixes to their problems.

“There's real satisfaction being part of a team which places so much importance on having the customer as Number One.”

Since October 2021, Gary has worked in-house in the Parts Department in the new TDX facility on Te Ngae Road. With retirement just around the corner, rather than sitting back and taking things easy, Gary has a number of plans on his radar.

“I'm all geared up and ready to go on to the next stage. I have seven grandchildren and I'm looking forward to having more time in their lives, whether it's during or after school, whenever - I can't wait to have more involvement with them.

“I'm also going to have a crack at a new business, selling two very different things - hearing aids and electric scooters! There will be enough for a couple of days work a week, so between the business and the grandkids I can see my days will be full.”

When asked if he had any words of advice for someone starting out at TDX, Gary paused for a moment before answering.

“You need to think about two things when you're in a sales role. The first is to value the customer's worth, the other is to sell yourself to the customer. If you can do both, you should do well.

"It's all about the relationship, that's the big thing, it's what we do every day.”

Good luck and best wishes for the future, Gary, and thanks for your contributions to TDX over more than 22 years.

 

TDX experience awaits Volvo contingent on big trip Down Under


The largest contingent of Volvo executives to ever visit TDX will be winging their way 'down under' at the end of this month on a flying visit to meet the team.

The travelling Volvo representatives will be made up of Bertrand Collette - Head of Market Area Oceania; Hanna Ihnatovich - CFO Region International; Greger Svanstrom - Head of Customer Financing and Electromobility Region International and Daniel Campos - Head of Retail Development Region International.

“It's not every day anyone from Volvo Head Office visits us here in New Zealand, let alone four Senior Executives all at once,” said Marc Warr, Marketing Manager.

“It's a considerable time investment and speaks volumes of how much Volvo values the relationship with TDX.”

The Volvo contingent will travel around the country as they meet our people, calling on a selection of TDX branches including Tauranga, Taupo and Christchurch.

“The Volvo team is keen to see how we represent their product locally, as well as gain a better understanding of the New Zealand market and to visit some key customers,” said Marc.


“It is also a chance to chat about future products, services and innovations - especially with an eye on emobility solutions.

“Above all else, it's an opportunity for TDX to demonstrate who we are and how we conduct business, and how much we value the relationship with Volvo and level of positive engagement between the brands.

“They'll be running a very busy schedule, which means plenty of time spent with our visitors,” said Marc.

“This trip will be invaluable, as nothing beats spending time with people, and that's the same for people you do business with.

“It's an opportunity to get to know someone and build a genuine connection. It makes doing business so much easier when you have a personal shared relationship with someone on the other side of the world, rather than just a name on the end of an email or a line on the end of a report.”

Top tech on the move up at TDX

Salman Hasan has been part of the TDX team for five years and has established himself as one of the top techs at TDX Auckland.




His hard work, dedication and excellent product knowledge has seen him recognised with a recent promotion, moving from the role of Field Service Technician (a position he held since starting at TDX in 2018) to Field Service Foreman in May this year.

“Salman finished his mechanical apprenticeship at Porter Mechanical, so came to TDX with a really good background covering a wide range of skills,” said Warren Peffer, Operations Manager - Upper North Island.

“Since that time, he has gained extensive experience and knowledge on the Volvo product.

“His promotion was well deserved and was due to his technical ability as well as being well respected by his peers.

“Has done an excellent job since going into the position and has made a positive contribution in planning work and supporting the technicians in the field.

“Salman's achievement is another example of TDX recognising talent and promoting from within the business.”

When he's not at work, Salman enjoys spending time with his wife and children, a two-year old girl and a new, five-month old boy, and also indulging in a spot of four-wheel driving.

Congratulations on your new role, Salman - nice work!
